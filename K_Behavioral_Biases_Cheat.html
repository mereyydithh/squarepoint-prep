K — BEHAVIORAL BIASES — CHEAT SHEET

7 Core Biases (PMs & Traders)

# Bias PM version Trader version Detection
1 Anchoring Won't sell below entry price Won't take a loss on fill price "Would you buy this TODAY?"
2 Confirmation Re-interprets all data as bullish Reads order flow selectively "What would make you wrong?"
3 Loss aversion Holds losers, cuts winners Revenge trading, refuses stops Avg win:loss ratio < 0.5
4 Disposition effect Sells winners early, holds losers Scalps 5 wins, lets 1 loss run Hold period: winners < losers
5 Overconfidence "90% probability" → 60% hit rate "I feel the market today" Conviction-outcome calibration
6 Recency Sizes up in low vol regime Sizes on trailing streak Size vs trailing P&L R² > 0.3
7 Thesis refitting 4 thesis changes, same position "Now it's a mean-reversion trade" Monthly thesis audit

5 Trader-Specific Biases

Bias What it is P&L signature
Revenge trading Increasing size after a loss to "make it back" Good AM → cliff PM
Action bias Trading when correct action is to wait High turnover + flat P&L
Gambler's fallacy "Down 5 days → due for a bounce" Counter-trend losses
Hot hand fallacy Sizing up on a win streak Oversized loss breaks streak
Sunk cost "I've spent $350K rolling — can't close now" Escalating committed capital

RM Self-Biases

Bias Danger Fix
Authority Defer to senior PM Document concern IN WRITING
Outcome "PM made money so process was fine" Evaluate process, not result
Normalcy "Never happened → won't happen" Run reverse stress tests
Complacency Green dashboard 6 months → stop reading Rotate focus weekly
Anchoring to models "VaR says $4M so $4M is risk" Triangulate: VaR + ES + stress + concentration

De-Biasing — Structural Countermeasures

Bias Fix
Anchoring Pre-trade thesis sheet with exit criteria
Confirmation Mandatory devil's advocate on Tier 1 positions
Loss aversion Mechanical stops — remove human from exit
Disposition Quarterly "keep 5 of 15" portfolio clean-out
Overconfidence Position limits requiring IC override
Recency Regime-aware stress tests (2008, 2020, 2022)
Thesis refitting Monthly thesis audit vs original
Revenge trading Mandatory pause at 50% daily budget consumed
Action bias Track turnover-to-P&L ratio
Herding 13F overlap + short interest monitor

Communication — Do's and Don'ts

Do say Don't say
"Would you buy this today at $88?" "You're being biased"
"The thesis has evolved A→B→C. Would you put this on fresh?" "You're loss-averse"
"Size is up 40% today — opportunity or morning P&L?" "You should cut this"
"What's the strongest bear case?" "I told you so"

Right-and-Early vs Wrong-and-Stubborn

Right-and-early Wrong-and-stubborn
Thesis Same as inception Changed 2+ times
Catalyst Specific, not yet invalidated Original catalyst passed or failed
Language Specific: "15% EPS beat on margin expansion" Vague: "it's just cheap"
Fresh-eyes test "Yes, I'd buy this today" Hesitation or narrative shift

Quick-Fire Interview Answers

  • Detecting bias: "I look at DATA patterns: thesis changes, win/loss size ratios, post-loss sizing, hold-period asymmetry"
  • Right-and-early vs wrong: "Same thesis + specific catalyst not invalidated vs thesis changed 2+ times + vague language"
  • Own biases: "Outcome bias (evaluate process not P&L), authority bias (document and hold ground), normalcy bias (proactive stress tests)"
  • Communication: "Surface the pattern without accusing — data-driven questions, not labels"
