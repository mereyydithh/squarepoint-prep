U — SCENARIO ANALYSIS

Facts → Exposure → Scenarios → Communication → Recommendation

Depth: 🔴 DEEP

WHY THIS MATTERS

This is the skill that separates you from a spreadsheet. When oil spikes 8% overnight or a PM is down 15% in a week, the fund needs someone who can process the situation in minutes — not hours — and communicate clearly what it means and what to do.

PART 1: THE FRAMEWORK

Every scenario follows the same 5 steps:

Step 1: FACTS — What actually happened?

Gather data. Don't react to headlines — check the numbers. How much did it move? Since when? Was it a gap or a grind? What else moved with it?

Step 2: EXPOSURE — What are we exposed to?

Pull up the book. What positions are affected — directly and indirectly? What's the net exposure? What are the Greeks telling you?

Step 3: SCENARIOS — What happens next?

Run 3 scenarios: base case / upside / downside. Don't just look at where we are — look at where we could be. "If this continues to [X], our P&L goes to [Y]."

Step 4: COMMUNICATION — One page, PM-ready.

Situation (2 sentences), current P&L impact ($), key risk (1 sentence), scenarios (3 bullets), recommendation (1 sentence). That's it. No essays.

Step 5: RECOMMENDATION — What should we do?

Not "we should hedge" but "I recommend buying 2-week SPX 6400 puts at $8.50 to cap our downside at -$3M in the escalation scenario. Cost: $170K."

PART 2: SCENARIO PLAYBOOK

Scenario 1: "Oil is up 8% overnight on a Hormuz escalation."

FACTS:
- Brent from $111 to ~$120. Major move — 99th percentile daily move.
- Check: what else moved? Gold up (safe haven)? Equities futures down? EM FX? Rates?
- Was it a newsflow gap (overnight tweet) or sustained buying? Check volume.

EXPOSURE:
- Direct: Any energy longs (XOM, SLB, etc.) — mark-to-market gain. Any airline/transport shorts — gain. Any energy-importing EM FX shorts — gain.
- Indirect: Broad equity exposure — likely negative (oil shock = growth concern). Rates positions — inflation spike pushes yields up, so long duration loses.
- Greeks: If holding commodity options, what happened to implied vol? Probably spiked → vega impact.
- Margin: 8% oil move will increase margin requirements on any oil-related futures/options.

SCENARIOS:

Scenario Oil Equities Rates Portfolio impact
Base: Diplomatic noise, calms down in 48h Back to $112-115 Recovers Stable Give back today's energy gains, limited damage
Escalation: Hormuz further restricted $125-135 -3-5% +20-30bps (inflation) Energy wins but rest of book gets hit. Net depends on positioning.
Full blockade: Strait closed $140-160 -8-12% Unclear (deflation fears vs inflation) Potential crisis — margin calls, liquidity dry-up, correlation spike

COMMUNICATION (one page to PM):

Oil moved +8% overnight on Hormuz escalation reports. Our net P&L is +$X from energy longs. However, equity book is -$Y and short puts have moved against us. Key risk: escalation scenario to $130+ takes total portfolio down $Z and breaches 4% drawdown trigger. Recommend: consider taking partial profit on energy longs to de-risk, and add SPX put protection at 6400 strike to cap equity downside. Cost: $NNK.

RECOMMENDATION: Specific, actionable, with cost.

Scenario 2: "PM is down 15% in a week."

FACTS:
- Which PM? What are their positions? Is this a single-name event or broad market?
- -15% from what base? If they started the month at +10% and are now at -5% MTD, the context is different from -15% on top of prior losses.
- What drove it? Identify the P&L attribution: delta loss? Gamma acceleration? Vol move? Specific event?

EXPOSURE:
- Is this idiosyncratic (one position blew up) or systematic (the whole book moved together)?
- Check: are other PMs also down? If yes, correlation problem. If no, this PM's specific thesis failed.
- What's remaining delta? Gamma? Did the loss change the risk profile of remaining positions?

SCENARIOS:

Scenario What happens Portfolio impact
Recovery: Thesis intact, position mean-reverts P&L recovers to -5%. PM vindicated.
Status quo: Position stays around current level PM stuck at -15%, burning theta if holding options. Dead capital.
Further deterioration: Thesis proven wrong -20-25%. Hard drawdown limit breached. Forced liquidation.

KEY QUESTIONS TO ASK THE PM:
1. "Is the thesis the same as when you put on the position? Walk me through it."
2. "What catalyst are you waiting for? When?"
3. "If you didn't have this position today, would you put it on at current levels?"
4. "Where's your stop? Has it changed?"

DECISION TREE:

Is PM at or beyond hard drawdown limit?
  YES → Mandatory reduction. Escalate to IC.
  NO  → Is PM approaching soft limit (>80%)?
    YES → PM conversation. Document thesis. Set review date.
    NO  → Thesis still intact? Catalyst still valid?
      YES → Monitor closely. Tighten surveillance.
      NO  → Recommend reduction. Flag to IC.

Scenario 3: "Portfolio vol is running 30% above target."

FACTS:
- Is it a spike (last 2 days) or a persistent trend (last 2 weeks)?
- What's the target? What's current realized?
- Is VIX (market vol) also elevated, or is this portfolio-specific?

DECOMPOSITION (the critical step):
- Market vol contribution: if SPX realized vol went from 15% to 22%, that's a 47% increase in market vol. If your beta is 1.0, your portfolio vol should rise proportionally. So ~47% of the excess is "not your fault."
- Portfolio-specific: what's left after accounting for market vol? That's position concentration, leverage, or correlation-driven. THAT'S actionable.

SCENARIOS:

Source What to do
Market vol (broad regime shift) Accept it, or reduce beta. Can't hedge the market being volatile.
Single position (one name is 3x more volatile than normal) Trim or hedge that specific position.
Correlation (positions that were diversified are now moving together) The structural problem. Reduce overlapping exposures or add truly uncorrelated positions.
Leverage (same positions, more margin) Reduce leverage. Simple but painful.

RECOMMENDATION FORMAT:
"Vol is 30% above target. Decomposition: 60% from market vol increase (SPX realized at 22% vs 15% trailing), 40% from XOM concentration (running 45% annualized vol vs book average of 18%). To bring vol back to target, options: (A) trim XOM by 25%, or (B) add a 2-week put spread on XOM to cap the downside, or (C) get IC approval to run elevated vol. I recommend (A) — it's clean and immediate."

Scenario 4: "The Fed holds rates (market expected a cut)."

FACTS:
- Market was pricing ~55% chance of a cut. Now getting a hold + hawkish statement.
- Immediate market reaction: equities -2%, 2Y yield +15bps, 10Y +10bps, dollar strengthens, gold drops.

EXPOSURE:
- Equity longs: immediate mark-to-market loss.
- Duration longs (if any): loss from rate rise.
- Short puts: as equities drop, short gamma accelerates losses.
- Dollar shorts: loss from dollar strength.
- Commodity longs: mixed — oil may drop on growth fears, but energy stocks could be hit by broad sell-off.

SCENARIOS:

Scenario Next 1-2 weeks Portfolio
Base: Market sells off 2-3% then stabilizes Modest loss, recoverable Monitor, no urgent action
Repricing: Market takes out 2+ future cuts — rates grind higher Sustained equity pressure, 5-7% decline over weeks Need to reduce equity exposure and adjust duration
Panic: Fed seen as behind the curve, stagflation narrative takes hold VIX spikes to 30+, credit spreads widen, correlation spike Full de-risk mode. Contact all PMs. Emergency IC call.

Scenario 5: "Correlation spike — all PMs drawing down simultaneously."

FACTS:
- 3 out of 4 PMs are down on the same day. Books look "diversified" (different sectors) but all bleeding.
- Run factor attribution: is there a common factor? Oil? Rates? Momentum? Dollar?

THIS IS THE MOST DANGEROUS SCENARIO. Because:
- Each PM thinks their loss is idiosyncratic ("my stock had bad earnings")
- But the aggregate loss is correlated to a single factor
- Portfolio-level risk is 3x worse than the sum of individual risks
- This is exactly what killed multi-strategy funds in Q1 2026

FRAMEWORK:
1. Factor decomposition: PCA or simpler — regress each PM's P&L against SPX, oil, 10Y, DXY. Find the common driver.
2. Aggregate exposure: sum everyone's effective oil beta (or whatever the common factor is). "Collectively, we have $400M of effective oil exposure across 4 PMs who each think they're running $100M of independent risk."
3. Communicate: "You each appear to have independent books but you're all loading on the same factor. Here's the data."
4. Recommend: either reduce the aggregate exposure or accept it consciously at the IC level.

PART 3: THE META-SKILL

What All Scenarios Have in Common

  1. Don't panic. The first 5 minutes are for GATHERING DATA, not acting.
  2. Decompose before recommending. "What's causing this?" comes before "what should we do?"
  3. Three scenarios, always. Base / adverse / extreme. Never just one.
  4. Quantify everything. Not "this is bad" but "this costs $X under scenario Y."
  5. Communicate fast, precisely, once. One message with everything the PM needs. Not five messages over an hour.
  6. Document. Whatever happens, write down: what occurred, what you flagged, what was decided, what the outcome was. When the IC asks "why didn't anyone flag this?" — you have your answer.
