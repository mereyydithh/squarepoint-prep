B — DERIVATIVES CHEAT SHEET

ONE-LINERS

Concept One-liner
Derivative A contract whose value is derived from an underlying asset — lets you take exposure without owning it
Future A binding agreement to buy/sell at a set price on a set date — both sides OBLIGATED
Option The RIGHT (not obligation) to buy/sell at a set price by a set date — premium is the price of that right
Call Right to BUY at the strike. Want underlying UP.
Put Right to SELL at the strike. Want underlying DOWN.
Swap OTC agreement to exchange cash flows over time — typically fixed for floating
IRS Exchange fixed-rate for floating-rate payments. Most traded derivative in the world.
TRS Get total economic exposure (price + dividends) WITHOUT owning the asset — synthetic leverage (Archegos)
CDS Insurance against default — buyer pays premium, seller pays if default occurs
Variance swap Pure vol bet — pays realized variance minus strike. Short variance = unlimited downside (Volmageddon)
Implied vol The vol the market is pricing into an option — backed out from the market price via Black-Scholes
Put-call parity C − P = Forward. A call minus a put = synthetic forward position. Arbitrage relationship.

OPTION TYPES & PRICING MODELS

Type Exercise Pricing Model Where Used Cost vs European
European Only at expiry Black-Scholes (closed-form) Index options (SPX), FX, most OTC Baseline
American Any time before expiry Binomial tree (check early exercise at each node) US single-stock options (AAPL, MSFT) ≥ European (early exercise right has value)
Bermudan Specific dates (e.g., quarterly) Lattice / Monte Carlo Swaptions, callable bonds Between European & American
Asian At expiry, payoff on AVERAGE price Monte Carlo (path-dependent) Commodity hedging, FX hedging < European (averaging reduces vol)
Barrier Knock-in/out at a price level Analytical or Monte Carlo Structured products, FX, commodities Cheaper (conditional activation)
Digital At expiry, fixed payout if ITM Modified BS Structured products Depends on structure

Why different models? BS works when you know WHEN exercise happens. Add optionality about WHEN (American) or path-dependency (Asian) → need numerical methods (trees, simulation).

OPTION VALUE

Price = Intrinsic Value + Time Value

Component Call Put
Intrinsic max(0, S − K) max(0, K − S)
Time value Premium − Intrinsic Premium − Intrinsic

Moneyness: Deep ITM (δ ≈ ±0.90–1.00) → ITM → ATM (δ ≈ ±0.50, max gamma/time value) → OTM → Deep OTM (δ ≈ ±0.01–0.10)

BLACK-SCHOLES

Inputs: Spot (S), Strike (K), Time (T), Rate (r), Dividend yield (q), Volatility (σ)
Output: Option price + Greeks

Where it breaks:
- Fat tails (crashes more frequent than bell curve)
- Vol isn't constant (vol surface exists)
- No continuous trading (gaps on earnings, weekends)
- No early exercise (need binomial for American)

SINGLE-LEG STRATEGIES

Strategy View Max Gain Max Loss Greeks
Long Call Bullish Unlimited Premium +Δ, +Γ, −Θ, +ν
Short Call Neutral/Bearish Premium Unlimited −Δ, −Γ, +Θ, −ν
Long Put Bearish Strike − premium Premium −Δ, +Γ, −Θ, +ν
Short Put Neutral/Bullish Premium Strike − premium +Δ, −Γ, +Θ, −ν

Pattern: Long options = +Γ, +ν, −Θ (pay rent, get protection). Short options = −Γ, −ν, +Θ (collect rent, bear tail risk). ALWAYS.

MULTI-LEG STRATEGIES

Strategy Construction View Key Insight
Bull call spread Buy low-K call + sell high-K call Moderately bullish Caps upside, reduces cost. Defined risk.
Bear put spread Buy high-K put + sell low-K put Moderately bearish Caps profit, cheaper than outright.
Straddle Buy ATM call + ATM put Big move, direction unknown Most expensive vol bet. Double theta.
Strangle Buy OTM call + OTM put Big move, cheaper than straddle Wider breakevens, needs bigger move.
Collar Long stock + buy put + sell call Protect downside, cap upside Can be zero-cost. Most common hedge.
Covered call Long stock + sell OTM call Income on existing position Synthetically = short put (put-call parity).
Butterfly Buy 1 low + sell 2 mid + buy 1 high Pin at middle strike Cheap defined-risk low-vol bet.
Iron condor Bear call spread + bull put spread Range-bound Defined-risk premium selling.
Risk reversal Sell OTM put + buy OTM call Strong directional Zero/near-zero cost. Measures skew.
Calendar spread Sell near-dated + buy far-dated (same K) Theta decay play Near decays faster than far.

WHAT KILLS FUNDS — DERIVATIVE RISKS

Risk What happens Example
Leverage $500K premium controls $20M notional. Losses scale to notional. Barings (Leeson), futures leverage
Non-linearity (gamma) Losses ACCELERATE: delta × move + gamma × move²/2 Any short gamma blowup
Liquidity illusion Tight spreads in calm → no quotes in stress OTC derivatives in March 2020
Model dependence Model wrong → "hedged" position isn't hedged Feb 2018 vol models, LME nickel 2022
Margin cascade Vol up → margin up → forced selling → more vol → spiral Amaranth, MF Global
Counterparty risk Counterparty defaults → you lose unrealized gains AIG 2008 ($182B bailout)
