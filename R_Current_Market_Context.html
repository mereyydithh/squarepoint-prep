PART 7: WHAT I'D SAY IF ASKED

"What's happening in markets right now?"

"The dominant story is the Iran war and Hormuz closure. Brent is at $111, up 50% since the war started in late February. Tonight at 8 PM we have Trump's deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait — that's a binary event.

The broader picture is stagflation risk. We have cost-push inflation from oil flowing through to the real economy — ISM input prices are at 13-year highs. But growth is deteriorating: Eurozone PMIs are at 9-month lows, France services are contracting. Central banks are stuck — the Fed has 97.9% pricing for a cut on April 29, but if CPI comes in hot on April 11, that gets repriced violently.

What worries me most as a risk manager is that correlations are elevated. Stocks and bonds are moving together, so the traditional 60/40 hedge is broken. Q1 saw multi-strat funds like Balyasny and Caxton draw down because their normally uncorrelated books all correlated to the same oil/inflation factor.

The key risk window is April 29-30 when the Fed, ECB, and BOE all decide within 48 hours. If any of them surprise, the repricing will cascade across asset classes."

"What does the Fed do April 29?"

"The market is pricing a 25bp cut with 97.9% probability. I think a cut is likely because the growth data is deteriorating and the Fed will want to front-run a potential recession. But there's a meaningful risk they hold: if the April 11 CPI print shows oil shock pass-through, cutting into rising inflation would damage their credibility.

If they hold, the repricing will be violent — nearly 100% of the market expects a cut. Two-year yields spike, dollar strengthens, EM gets crushed, growth stocks sell off. For our book, I'd want to understand our rate sensitivity before the decision and have contingency plans for the hold scenario."
