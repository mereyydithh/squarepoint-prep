QUESTIONS TO ASK — BY ROUND AND INTERVIEWER

ROUND 1: PRE-SCREEN WITH RECRUITER (Solomon Price)

Solomon is a Recruitment Specialist — law background (Exeter + Rennes), not a risk or trading person. He's evaluating fit, motivation, and whether to pass you forward. Your questions should be practical and strategic — show you're serious, organized, and already thinking ahead.

Pick 2-3 max. Don't overdo it.

Q1: Team Structure

"How is the discretionary team structured today? How many people are on it, and where would this role sit — would I be reporting directly to the head of risk, or is there another layer?"

Why it works: Shows you're thinking about where you fit, not just whether you get the job. Also gives you intel for later rounds.

Q2: Interview Process

"What does the interview process look like from here — how many rounds, and who would I typically meet?"

Why it works: Practical, shows you're organized and ready to move. Also lets you prepare for what's coming.

Q3: New Headcount (with "Why Squarepoint" woven in)

"I noticed the JD talks about helping 'further develop' and 'drive the build out' of the discretionary risk framework — is this a new headcount tied to the growth of the discretionary business, or is it a backfill?"

Why it works: You're signaling that you've read the JD carefully AND that you understand this is a build-out phase. It implicitly says "I'm excited about building, not just maintaining." The answer also tells you how much greenfield work there actually is.

Q4: Location Preference (only if it hasn't come up)

"The role lists New York and London — is there a preference for where this person sits, or is it flexible?"

Why it works: Gets clarity on visa dynamics without making it awkward. If they say London, you can pivot: "I'm open to both — I wouldn't need sponsorship for London as an EU citizen."

Q5: Timeline

"What's the timeline you're working with? Is there urgency to fill the role?"

Why it works: Shows you can move fast if needed. Also gives you a read on how competitive the process is.

CLOSER (use after your last question — always end with this)

"I just want to say — I'm genuinely excited about this. The combination of buy-side risk, a discretionary team that's actively being built out, and Squarepoint's quantitative infrastructure is exactly what I've been targeting since my time in New York. I'd love to move forward."

Why it works: You leave zero ambiguity about your motivation. Solomon's job is to gauge enthusiasm — give him a clear signal.

ROUND 2: HIRING MANAGER / HEAD OF RISK

This is where you show depth. These questions demonstrate you already think like a risk manager.

Q1: Risk Allocation Framework

"How are risk budgets currently allocated across the discretionary strategies? Is it a top-down allocation from the IC, or do PMs propose and the risk team approves?"

Why it works: This is a core mechanic of the role. Shows you understand discretionary risk isn't just monitoring — it's negotiation and governance.

Q2: Scaling Challenge (with "Why Squarepoint" woven in)

"Squarepoint has grown significantly — from 60 people at the spin-off to over 700 today. As the discretionary business scales alongside the systematic side, what's the biggest risk management challenge you see? Is it infrastructure, process, or talent?"

Why it works: You're referencing the firm's growth trajectory (shows research), asking about pain points (shows you want to solve problems), and framing it in a way that lets them sell the role to you.

Q3: Day-to-Day Rhythm

"What does a typical day or week look like for the risk team on the discretionary side? How much is real-time monitoring versus proactive analysis versus building infrastructure?"

Why it works: You're figuring out the split between reactive and proactive work — which tells you how mature the function is and where your impact would be.

Q4: Interaction with Systematic Side

"One of the things that drew me to Squarepoint is the systematic infrastructure sitting alongside the discretionary team. In practice, how much does the discretionary risk team leverage quantitative tools or data from the systematic side?"

Why it works: This is a natural "Why Squarepoint" weave — you're saying the combination of systematic + discretionary is the reason you're here, AND you're asking a substantive question about how it actually works.

Q5: Success Criteria

"What would success look like in the first 6-12 months for someone in this role? What's the one thing you'd want them to have built or improved?"

Why it works: Forces them to tell you what they actually need. Also signals you're goal-oriented and want to deliver, not just show up.

Q6: Biggest Risk Right Now (only if vibe is right)

"Without getting into anything confidential — what keeps you up at night right now from a risk perspective? Is it macro, concentration, correlation, something structural?"

Why it works: This is a peer-level question that signals you already think about risk the way they do. Only use it if the conversation is flowing well and the HM seems open.

ROUND 3: TEAM MEMBERS / QUANT RESEARCHERS / TRADERS

These are the people you'd work with daily. Questions should show you're collaborative and understand their world.

Q1: Collaboration Dynamic

"How does the risk team typically interact with you day to day? Is it more proactive — like flagging something before a trade goes on — or reactive, like a daily risk report?"

Why it works: Shows you care about how risk actually works in practice, not just in theory. Also tells you the team's operating model.

Q2: Pushback

"Can you think of a time when the risk team identified something that changed how you sized or structured a position? I'm curious how that feedback loop works here."

Why it works: You're asking how much actual influence the risk function has. If they can't think of an example, that tells you something about the culture.

Q3: What They Want from Risk (with "Why Squarepoint" woven in)

"If you could design the ideal risk manager to sit with your team — what skill or quality would matter most? I'm asking because part of what attracted me to Squarepoint is the idea of being embedded with the people making decisions, not siloed in a separate function."

Why it works: You're getting intel on what they value AND expressing your motivation in a way that's specific to Squarepoint's setup.

Q4: Research Platform

"The role mentions leveraging Squarepoint's research platform — what does that look like in practice for the discretionary team? What data or tools do you have access to?"

Why it works: Shows genuine curiosity about the infrastructure and signals you'd actually use it, not just run standard reports.

ROUND 4: SENIOR LEADERSHIP / CRO / FINAL ROUND

Strategic questions. Show you think about the firm holistically.

Q1: Discretionary Strategy (with "Why Squarepoint" woven in)

"The discretionary build-out is clearly a strategic priority — what's the long-term vision? Is the goal to reach parity with the systematic side in AUM, or is it designed to be a complementary diversifier?"

Why it works: You're asking about strategic direction, which shows you're thinking beyond just getting the job. The implicit message: I want to be part of something that's growing.

Q2: Risk Culture

"How would you describe the risk culture at Squarepoint? In some funds, risk is a constraint function — it exists to say no. In others, it's a partner to the investment process. Where does Squarepoint sit?"

Why it works: This question reveals the firm's true operating model. Also signals you want to be a partner, not a cop.

Q3: Growth Path

"For someone coming in at the junior level and performing well — what does growth look like over 3-5 years? Would I specialize in a particular strategy, or is the path toward broader risk responsibility?"

Why it works: Shows you're thinking long-term and committed, not just looking for a stepping stone.

HOW TO WEAVE "WHY SQUAREPOINT" INTO ANY QUESTION

The formula is: [Specific Squarepoint fact] + [genuine question about it]

Don't say "I'm interested because..." — instead, embed the research into the question itself. Examples:

Instead of... Say...
"I'm interested in Squarepoint because of the discretionary build-out" "The discretionary team is expanding — is this headcount part of that growth?"
"I like that you have systematic infrastructure" "How much does the discretionary side leverage tools from the systematic platform?"
"I know you spun off from Barclays" "Since the 2014 spin-off, the firm has grown 10x — how has the risk function evolved with that?"
"I want to work in New York" "I used to live across the street from your NY office — is that where this role would be based?"

The rule: show, don't tell. Let the research speak through your questions rather than listing facts at them.
