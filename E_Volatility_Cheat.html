E — VOLATILITY CHEAT SHEET

CORE CONCEPTS

Concept Definition Key fact
Realized vol StdDev of actual returns × √252 Backward-looking. Lookback window matters (5d vs 20d vs 252d).
Implied vol Vol backed out of option prices (solve BS for σ) Forward-looking. Contains expected vol + VRP.
VRP (Vol Risk Premium) IV − subsequent RV +2-4 pts SPX average. Positive ~85% of months. Makes selling vol profitable on average, catastrophic in tails.

IV ≠ expected vol. IV = expected RV + insurance premium.

THE VOL SURFACE

Two dimensions: Strike (skew) × Expiry (term structure)

Skew (Strike Dimension)

  • Equities: "smirk" — OTM puts have HIGHER IV than ATM. Pre-1987 this didn't exist.
  • Why: demand for puts + fat tails + leverage effect
  • 25Δ risk reversal = IV(25Δ put) − IV(25Δ call). SPX typical: −5 to −8. More negative = steeper.
  • 25Δ butterfly = ½[IV(25Δ put) + IV(25Δ call)] − IV(ATM). Measures curvature/kurtosis.
  • Steep skew = fear. Flat skew = complacency.

Sticky-strike vs sticky-delta:
- Sticky-strike: vol stays at the strike → ATM vol rises when spot drops. Empirically correct for equities.
- Sticky-delta: vol stays at the delta → ATM vol unchanged when spot moves. More FX.
- Affects delta-hedging: different assumptions → different deltas.

Term Structure (Time Dimension)

Shape Name Meaning
Upward Contango Normal — more uncertainty over longer horizons
Flat Flat Transition
Inverted Backwardation Near-term fear (event risk). Market expects resolution.
Humped Humped Known event at specific future date

Asymmetry: Vol shifts UP in hours/days, shifts DOWN over weeks/months.

Vol clustering: Big moves follow big moves. After a 3σ day, expect more. Don't relax.

VIX

VIX = 30-day SPX expected vol from OTM option prices (model-free, not BS).

Daily move conversion: VIX / √252 → VIX 20 ≈ 1.26% daily move.

VIX Daily move Context
10-14 ±0.6-0.9% Complacency. Protection cheap — BUY IT.
15-20 ±0.9-1.3% Normal.
20-30 ±1.3-1.9% Elevated. Tighten limits.
30-40 ±1.9-2.5% Fear. Daily IC calls.
40-60 ±2.5-3.8% Severe stress.
60-80+ ±3.8-5.0%+ Existential. 2020 record = 82.69.

Properties: Negatively correlated with SPX (ρ ≈ −0.80). Mean-reverting. Non-tradeable directly.

VIX FUTURES & ETPs

VIX futures typically in contango. Front month = ~1-3 pts above spot VIX.
Beta to spot VIX: Front month ≈ 0.5. Back months ≈ 0.2-0.3.

Product Exposure Long-run fate
UVXY (2x long) 2× daily front-month → 0 (contango + rebalancing)
SVXY (−0.5x) −0.5× daily front-month Positive (harvests contango)
XIV (−1x) TERMINATED (Feb 5, 2018) Lost 96% in one day. Death spiral.

XIV lesson: Short vol + auto-rebalancing = convexly short vol → death spiral in a spike.

VOL PRODUCTS

Product Payoff Short risk
Straddle ~Linear in vol Capped-ish
Vol swap Linear in vol Limited
Variance swap Linear in variance (vol²) → CONVEX in vol UNLIMITED

Variance swap: RV of 40 vs strike of 20 → payoff on 1600−400=1200 of variance, NOT 40−20=20 of vol.

DEALER DYNAMICS

Dealer position Market effect Realized vol
Long gamma (normal) Buy dips + sell rallies → DAMPEN RV < IV
Short gamma (danger) Sell dips + buy rallies → AMPLIFY RV > IV

Gamma flip level (GEX): SPX price where dealer gamma switches sign.
- Above = suppressed, mean-reverting. Below = amplified, trending.

Vanna flows: Vol change → delta change → forced dealer hedging → billions daily.
Charm flows: Time decay → delta drift → systematic morning selling near OpEx.

0DTE: ~50% of SPX options volume. Massive intraday gamma. Pin/de-pin around round strikes. Traditional VaR doesn't capture this.

OPEX: Week before = suppressed (gamma pin). Week after = potentially explosive (gamma removed).

IMPLIED CORRELATION

$$\sigma_{\text{index}}^2 \approx \bar{\sigma}_{\text{stocks}}^2 \times \bar{\rho}$$

Correlation Meaning
20-35% Stocks independent. Stock-picking works. Diversification real.
40-55% Macro dominating. Sectors correlating.
60-80%+ Crisis. "All correlations → 1." Diversification illusion broken.

Dispersion trade: Short index vol + long stock vol = short correlation. Collect premium. Lose in crisis.

KEY INTERVIEW LINES

IV vs RV: "Implied is the forecast + insurance premium. Realized is the actual weather. The gap is the VRP — why selling vol is profitable on average and catastrophic in the tail."

Vol surface: "Skew = fear of crashes priced in. Term structure = how long the market expects current conditions to last. Together they determine every option price and every Greek."

Dealer gamma: "Long gamma = dealers buy dips, sell rallies → suppress vol. Short gamma = opposite → amplify. Below the gamma flip level, the market loses its shock absorber."

VIX: "Below 15 = buy protection while it's cheap. Above 30 = tighten everything. Backwardation = 'crisis is now.' Always check VVIX — uncertainty about uncertainty."

Variance swaps: "Linear in variance, convex in vol. A realized vol of 40 vs strike of 20 doesn't pay 2x — it pays on 1600 vs 400 of variance. That's why short var swap blew up XIV."
