Quick reference for correlation, diversification, factors, concentration, basis, tail, liquidity, construction, and the RM dashboard

CORRELATION

Formula: ρ = Cov(X,Y) / (σ_X · σ_Y). Range: −1 to +1.

Danger property What it means
Regime-dependent ρ from calm period ≠ ρ in crisis
Asymmetric ρ is HIGHER in sell-offs than rallies
Non-stationary Drifts even within a regime
Only linear ρ=0 ≠ independent (tail dependence can exist)
Estimation error 500 assets → 124,750 pairwise ρ → massive noise

Key table:

Pair Calm ρ Stress ρ
SPX vs single stocks 0.30 0.70+
SPX vs HY credit 0.50 0.85+
SPX vs UST 10Y −0.30 −0.50 (usually) BUT +0.40 in 2022
Cross-PM portfolios 0.10-0.20 0.50-0.80

Models: Rolling window → EWMA (λ=0.94) → DCC-GARCH → Factor-implied → Copula

Eigenvalue diagnostic: Dominant eigenvalue explains >60% → portfolio is ONE factor bet disguised as many positions.

DIVERSIFICATION

Formula: σ_p = σ √(1/N + (N−1)/N · ρ̄). Floor = σ√ρ̄ (can never diversify below this).

Diversification Ratio: DR = Σ(w_i · σ_i) / σ_p. DR → 1.0 = no diversification. DR > 1.5 = good.

Effective Number of Bets: ENB = DR² or Shannon entropy of eigenvalues.

N positions ρ=0.0 ρ=0.20 ρ=0.40 ρ=0.80
1 25% 25% 25% 25%
10 7.9% 12.1% 15.0% 21.1%
100 2.5% 11.2% 14.1% 20.7%
∞ 0% 11.2% 15.8% 22.4%

Most benefit captured by 15-20 uncorrelated positions. After that, reduce CORRELATION, not add names.

When diversification fails: Crisis → ρ spikes → DR drops → portfolio vol jumps → margin calls → forced selling → more ρ → SPIRAL.

FACTOR MODELS

Return decomposition: r_i = α + Σ β_ik · f_k + ε_i

Three families:

Type Examples Strengths Weaknesses
Fundamental (Barra/Axioma) Market, Size, Value, Momentum, Quality, Low Vol + sectors Pre-defined, interpretable Misses novel factors
Macro SPX, Δ10Y, ΔIG spread, ΔWTI, ΔDXY, ΔVIX Direct economic meaning May miss micro factors
Statistical (PCA) PC1 ≈ market, PC2 ≈ growth/value, PC3 ≈ size No assumptions, data-driven Hard to interpret, unstable

Factor risk contribution: Factor_k % = (β²_pk · σ²_k) / σ²_p

Alpha check: R² high + α ≈ 0 → PM is selling factor exposure at 2/20 fees.

CONCENTRATION RISK

HHI: Σ w_i². Effective N = 1/HHI.

HHI Effective N Level
0.01 100 Very diversified
0.05 20 Moderate
0.10 10 Concentrated
0.50+ ≤2 Single bet

Five types:

Type What it is How to detect
Single-name One stock dominates HHI, position % of NAV
Sector Same industry everywhere Sector weights
Factor Hidden common exposure Factor regression, PCA
Counterparty One PB holds too much PB margin allocation
Crowding Same trade across industry 13F overlap, short interest

Crowding blow-ups: Quant quake (Aug 2007), Short vol (Feb 2018), Momentum reversal (Sep 2020), Japan carry (Aug 2024).

BASIS RISK

Definition: Basis = hedged asset price − hedge instrument price. Unstable basis → hedge fails.

Hedge effectiveness: R² = 1 − Var(hedged) / Var(unhedged). R² > 0.95 = good. R² < 0.50 = not a hedge.

Optimal hedge ratio: h* = ρ × σ_position / σ_hedge. Naive 1:1 leaves basis risk.

Worst case: Lose on BOTH position AND hedge (2022: long IG bonds hedged with Treasuries → credit AND rates both lost).

TAIL RISK & FAT TAILS

Sigma event Normal says Reality Underestimation
3σ 1 in 370 days 1 in 50-70 days ~6×
4σ 1 in 126 years 1 in 2-3 years ~50×
5σ 1 in 3.5M days 1 in 20-30 years ~500×

Excess kurtosis: SPX daily ≈ 20-25. Normal = 0.

EVT (Generalized Pareto): Shape parameter ξ > 0 → power-law fat tails. Equities ξ ≈ 0.2-0.4.

Tail dependence: Gaussian copula has λ_L = 0 → MISSES joint crashes. Student-t or Clayton copula captures tail co-movement.

LIQUIDITY RISK

Market impact (Almgren-Chriss): Cost = ½·spread + η·speed + γ·(Q/ADV). Non-linear — 5× size costs 10-15× more.

Position/ADV Classification
<1% Liquid
1-5% Manageable
5-10% Illiquid — need plan
10-25% YOU are the market
>25% Trapped

Liquidity spiral (Brunnermeier-Pedersen): Losses → margin calls → forced selling → prices drop → more margin calls → FEEDBACK LOOP.

Tiering: Tier 1 (ADV >$100M, DTL <1d) → Tier 4 (bespoke OTC, DTL 20+d, full notional at risk).

INTERVIEW QUICK-FIRE

"Portfolio risk beyond VaR?" → "Five layers: ES + ES/VaR ratio for tail shape, factor decomposition for what's DRIVING risk, stress tests for regime breaks, liquidity analysis for exit capacity, cross-desk aggregation for hidden factor concentration. Most critical at a multi-PM fund: eigenvalue decomposition of inter-desk correlation — if first eigenvalue is >60%, your 'four independent desks' are actually one bet."

"Most underappreciated risk in a multi-PM fund?" → "Hidden factor correlation across PMs. Each PM is independent but they load on the same macro factors. In calm markets, idiosyncratic masks the common factor. In stress, correlations spike and you discover you have 1.5 effective bets, not 4. Fix: daily cross-desk factor aggregation + aggregate factor limits + effective-number-of-bets metric."

"Is a PM generating alpha or factor exposure?" → "Multi-factor regression. R² high + α ≈ 0 = selling factor as alpha. Need α positive + t-stat >2.0 + ~4 years of data to confirm skill."

"Build a risk dashboard from scratch?" → "Six layers: Exposure (size of bet) → Concentration (is it spread or stacked) → Factors (nature of bet) → Risk measures (how much can we lose) → Liquidity (can we exit) → Drawdown (where are we vs survival limits). Most important single metric: ENB from eigenvalue decomposition."
