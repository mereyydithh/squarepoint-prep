A2 — COMMODITIES CHEAT SHEET

ONE-LINERS

Concept One-liner
Physical Real barrels/tonnes changing hands for near-term delivery — producers, consumers, trading houses
Cash/Forward OTC contracts for physical delivery priced vs a benchmark
Futures Exchange-traded, standardized, margined — what financial players trade
Contango Front month cheaper than back months — storage costs. Negative roll yield (you bleed on every roll)
Backwardation Front month more expensive — tight supply now. Positive roll yield (you earn on every roll)
Crack spread Product price minus crude price — the refiner's gross margin
Spark spread Power price minus (gas price × heat rate) — the gas generator's margin
Crush spread (Soy meal + soy oil revenue) minus soybean cost — the processor's margin
Basis Price at a specific location vs the benchmark — reflects local supply/demand and logistics
Heat rate Efficiency of converting gas to electricity (MMBtu/MWh). Lower = more efficient
Merit order Generators dispatched cheapest to most expensive. The marginal unit sets the price for all
Roll yield Gain or loss from rolling an expiring futures contract to the next month — can dominate returns

CRUDE OIL BENCHMARKS

Benchmark Exchange Settlement What it represents
WTI NYMEX (CL) Physical @ Cushing, OK US domestic. Light sweet. Can go negative (did in April 2020).
Brent ICE (B) Cash-settled Global benchmark. ~75% of world crude priced off Brent.
Dated Brent OTC (Platts) Physical North Sea cargoes The PHYSICAL benchmark. Futures converge to this.
Dubai/Oman DME Physical (Oman crude) Asian sour crude benchmark. Prices Middle East → Asia flows.

Brent-WTI spread: Normally Brent +$2-5. Reflects Atlantic Basin balance. Blew out to $25 in 2011 (Cushing glut).

Crude quality: API gravity (higher = lighter = more valuable). Sulfur (sweet <0.5%, sour >0.5%). Light sweet commands a premium.

REFINED PRODUCTS

Product Exchange/Ticker Size Season What it is
RBOB Gasoline NYMEX (RB) 42,000 gal Summer (May-Sep) Motor fuel
ULSD (Diesel) NYMEX (HO) 42,000 gal Winter (heating) Diesel + heating oil
Jet Fuel OTC (vs Platts/ULSD) No standard Year-round Aviation fuel, priced as crack to ULSD
Fuel Oil (HSFO/VLSFO) ICE, SGX Various Year-round Shipping fuel. IMO 2020 split into VLSFO (0.5% S) and HSFO (3.5% S)

Crack spreads:
- Simple: Product − Crude
- 3-2-1: (2 × RBOB + 1 × ULSD − 3 × Crude) / 3 = US Gulf refiner margin
- Wide cracks → refiners maximize throughput → pulls crude demand
- 2022 diesel cracks hit $70+/bbl (normal $15-25) — Russia sanctions

NATURAL GAS — THREE SEPARATE MARKETS

Region Benchmark Exchange Unit Price Range
US Henry Hub (HH) NYMEX (NG) $/MMBtu ~$2-4 (cheap, shale)
Europe TTF ICE Endex €/MWh Volatile ($30-340 in 2022)
UK NBP ICE p/therm Trades vs TTF spread
Asia JKM (Platts) OTC $/MMBtu Competes with TTF for LNG cargoes

Conversion: 1 MMBtu ≈ 0.293 MWh. $3/MMBtu ≈ €9.5/MWh (rough).

Key concepts:
- Associated gas: Gas from oil drilling. More oil drilling → more gas → gas prices FALL even as energy demand rises
- Storage report: EIA every Thursday 10:30 AM ET. Market trades deviation from consensus.
- LNG arbitrage: Cargoes flow to whoever pays more — JKM > TTF → cargoes go to Asia; TTF > JKM → cargoes to Europe
- Oil-indexed LNG: Asian long-term contracts priced as ~14.5% of JCC crude. $80 oil → $11.60 gas.
- TTF became global LNG benchmark after 2022 energy crisis

US Gas Hubs — Delivery Locations

Hub Where What it reflects Basis to HH
Henry Hub Erath, Louisiana THE benchmark. 13 pipelines converge. —
Waha West Texas (Permian) Oversupplied associated gas, pipeline-constrained Deep discount (sometimes negative)
Dominion South Appalachia (Marcellus) Shale gas production, pipeline bottlenecks Discount
Algonquin Citygate New England Northeast heating demand. Winter spikes. Massive winter premium ($10-30+ in polar vortex)
SoCal Citygate Southern California West Coast power generation Moderate premium
Chicago Citygate Midwest Heating + industrial. Well-connected. Small premium or parity
AECO Alberta, Canada Canadian gas, depends on US export pipes Discount
Transco Zone 6 NYC delivery Urban Northeast. Extreme winter premiums. Premium
Katy Hub SE Texas Gulf Coast industrial + LNG export demand Near parity

Why basis matters: In the 2014 polar vortex, HH = $4.50 while Algonquin = $78 — a 17x difference. Hedging New England exposure with HH futures covers almost nothing when pipelines are maxed.

LNG export terminals: Sabine Pass (~4.2 Bcf/d), Freeport (~2 Bcf/d), Cameron, Calcasieu Pass, Corpus Christi. Feed gas data (gas flowing into terminals) is watched daily — drops signal operational issues.

US POWER MARKETS

ISO/RTO Region Key Feature
PJM Mid-Atlantic + Midwest Largest US market. Capacity market. Coal/gas mix.
ERCOT Texas (isolated) NO capacity market → extreme spikes. $9,000/MWh in Feb 2021 freeze.
CAISO California Solar-heavy. Duck curve: oversupply midday, spike at sunset.
NYISO New York Congested transmission. NYC ≠ upstate prices.
ISO-NE New England Winter gas/power spikes (pipeline constraints).
MISO Midwest Wind-heavy. Low prices when windy.
SPP Central Plains Very wind-heavy. Negative prices when oversupplied.

Key formulas:
- Implied heat rate = Power price / Gas price → profitability of burning gas
- Spark spread = Power − (Gas × Heat rate) → generator margin. Negative = uneconomic to run.
- Dark spread = Power − (Coal × Emission cost) → coal generator margin.

Negative prices: Power can go negative when renewables overproduce and generators pay to stay connected.

LME is unique: 3-month benchmark, daily prompt dates, physical delivery via LME warehouses, open-outcry ring.

COMEX Copper: 25,000 lbs. Cents/lb. Convert: LME $/tonne ÷ 2,204.6 × 100 = cents/lb.

Doctor Copper: Leading growth indicator — copper goes into everything (wiring, plumbing, EVs, grids).

2022 Nickel squeeze: Tsingshan short → Russia war spiked nickel → $25K to $100K in 24 hours → LME cancelled trades → credibility destroyed.

Gold is NOT an inflation hedge — it's a REAL RATE hedge. Watch 10yr TIPS yield: falls → gold rises. Rises → gold falls.

RM RED FLAGS — COMMODITIES

Red Flag Why it matters
Position >5% of open interest You ARE the market — liquidation moves prices against you
Large position near expiry, no roll plan Operational disaster — especially physically-settled (WTI)
Large long in deep contango Bleeding roll cost every month regardless of spot
"Diversified" commodities that are all growth bets Long crude + NatGas + copper = one macro bet, not three
Unmonitored basis risk Hedging jet fuel with WTI ≠ hedged
Margin increases during vol Forces liquidation at worst time
Geopolitical binary (Hormuz, Black Sea) Overnight gaps with no hedge
Fighting seasonality without thesis Long NatGas in April needs a strong reason
