C — MARKET MECHANICS CHEAT SHEET

ONE-LINERS

Concept One-liner
Trade lifecycle Order → route → execute → clear (CCP novation) → settle (delivery vs payment)
Market order Guaranteed execution, NOT guaranteed price. Can cross multiple levels in a thin book.
Limit order Guaranteed price, NOT execution. You may never get filled.
CCP Steps between buyer and seller → eliminates bilateral risk but creates concentration risk
Novation CCP replaces original contract with two: A→CCP and CCP→B
Settlement US equities T+1 (since May 2024). European equities T+2. FX spot T+2.
ISDA Constitution of OTC derivatives — governs netting, close-out, events of default
CSA Collateral framework — thresholds, eligible collateral, haircuts, rehypothecation
Initial margin Deposit to OPEN a position. Performance bond.
Variation margin Daily mark-to-market settlement. Losing side pays.
Maintenance margin Minimum level. Below → margin call (post to IM, not maintenance).
Pro-cyclical margin Exchanges RAISE margin in vol spikes → demand cash when you can least afford it
Margin cascade Margin call → forced selling → price drops → more margin calls → spiral
Repo Collateralized short-term loan. Sell securities today, agree to buy back tomorrow.
Haircut Discount on collateral: post $100M bonds, receive $98M cash (2% haircut).
Gross leverage (Longs + Shorts) / NAV → total exposure
Net leverage (Longs − Shorts) / NAV → directional exposure
Market liquidity Can you sell at fair value in size? Bid-ask, depth, ADV, impact.
Funding liquidity Can you access cash? Credit lines, repo, margin availability.
Liquidity spiral Market illiquidity → funding pressure → forced selling → more illiquidity → doom loop
Short squeeze Forced buying feedback loop — shorts cover → price rises → more shorts forced to cover
Days to cover Short interest / ADV. Above 10 = crowded short, squeeze risk.
Gamma flip Price where dealer gamma goes from positive (dampening) to negative (amplifying moves)

ISDA / CSA KEY TERMS

Term What it means
Netting Multiple trades → single net amount if default. Reduces exposure 80-90%.
Close-out All trades terminated and netted on credit event. No cherry-picking.
Threshold Below this, no collateral required. Higher = more unsecured exposure.
MTA Minimum transfer amount. Small moves ignored.
Haircut Discount on non-cash collateral.
Rehypothecation Receiver re-uses your collateral. Efficient but risky (Lehman trapped $65B+).
SIMM ISDA's standard model for bilateral IM calculation. Sensitivity-based.

Model Used by Method
SPAN CME, futures exchanges 16 scenarios, worst-case loss
TIMS OCC (equity options) Scenario-based
SIMM Bilateral OTC (ISDA) Sensitivity-based (delta, vega, curvature)
VaR-based Prime brokers Historical/MC VaR, 99%, 10-day

REPO MECHANICS

Term Detail
Repo rate ≈ SOFR for UST repo
Haircut UST: 1-2%. IG corp: 3-5%. HY: 10-25%. Equity: 15-25%.
Term Mostly overnight (70%+). Also 1W, 1M, 3M, open.
GC General collateral — any acceptable security
Special Specific bond demanded — lower repo rate (scarce collateral)
Reverse repo Mirror: LEND cash, receive securities

Leverage via repo: Buy $100M bonds → repo for $99M → buy more → repo again → 50x leverage on $2M equity. A 2% move = total wipeout.

LEVERAGE PROFILES

Profile Gross Net Interpretation
Market neutral 6x 0.2x Lots of positions, little directional risk
Levered directional 6x 4x Maximum risk
Conviction, low lev 1.5x 1.2x Concentrated longs, modest leverage
Conservative hedged 1.5x 0.1x Low leverage, well hedged

Better than notional: Risk-adjusted leverage, DV01/NAV, marginal VaR leverage, effective leverage (gross × beta).

Why leverage kills: -50% needs +100% to recover. -75% needs +300%. Margin calls force selling AT the bottom.

LIQUIDITY TIERING

Tier Exit time Examples
Tier 1 < 1 day G10 FX, SPY, index futures, on-the-run UST
Tier 2 1-5 days Large-cap stocks, IG bonds, liquid options
Tier 3 5-20 days Small-cap, HY bonds, EM credit, off-the-run UST
Tier 4 > 20 days Distressed debt, exotic OTC, private placements

Red flag: Tier 3+4 > 15% NAV for open-ended fund = redemption mismatch.

DEALER GAMMA

Dealer position They do Market effect
Long gamma Buy dips, sell rallies DAMPENS moves, range-bound
Short gamma Sell declines, buy rallies AMPLIFIES moves, vol spikes

Gamma flip: Below this level → dealer short gamma → sell-offs accelerate.

SHORT SELLING

Component Detail
Process Locate → borrow → sell → wait → cover (buy back & return)
Borrow fee Easy: 0.25-0.50%. Hard-to-borrow: 5-50%+
You pay Borrow fee + dividends to lender
You earn Short rebate on restricted cash proceeds
Max loss Unlimited (price can rise infinitely)
Buy-in risk Lender recalls → forced cover at market

Squeeze signals: SI > 20% float, days-to-cover > 10, borrow utilization > 90%, rising borrow cost.

RM RED FLAGS

Metric Red flag
Margin utilization > 70% in calm markets
Stressed margin > 90% at VIX +20
Gross leverage > 4x for discretionary
Days-to-close (DTC) Any position > 5 days
Tier 3+4 % NAV > 15% for open-ended fund
Overnight repo funding > 50% on long-dated assets
Borrow utilization (shorts) > 80% on any position
Bid-ask vs 30d avg > 2x normal = liquidity deteriorating
