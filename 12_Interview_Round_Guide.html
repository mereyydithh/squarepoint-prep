THE 4 ROUNDS

ROUND 1: Discretionary Trader (6-12 month onboarding partner)

What they said: You'd work with this person for 6-12 months during onboarding. Think of it as "would I want to sit next to this person every day?"

What they're testing:
- Market awareness — do you understand their world?
- Real-world responses — how would you react to live scenarios?
- Can you communicate risk in a way that's useful to a trader?
- Cultural fit — do they want to work with you?

How to prepare:
- Files 08 (Asset Classes), 09 (Market Intuition), 10 (Market Context Today)
- File 06 (Inside Discretionary Fund) — know the daily rhythm
- File 16 (Probe Defense) — they'll ask about your experience in detail
- Be ready for: "I just put on X position — what do you think?" or "What risk would you flag here?"
- Don't try to be the trader. Show you understand their thesis AND can add the risk lens they don't have.

ROUND 2: Senior Risk Manager (one of the 4 current seniors)

What they said: Career experience discussion, key risk concepts. Nothing overly technical.

What they're testing:
- Do you actually understand risk management (not just definitions)?
- Career story coherence — does your path make sense?
- Key risk concepts: VaR vs ES, stress testing philosophy, correlation, concentration, tail risk
- Judgment — when do you escalate vs. hold the line?

How to prepare:
- File 01 (PreScreen Answers) — sharpen the walkthrough for a risk audience
- File 07 (Risk Reference) — know the concepts cold but explain them simply
- File 13 (Story Bank) — have 3-4 stories ready (fire drill, stress model, skew scanner, mentor lesson)
- File 14 (Hedge Fund Mechanics) — fund structure, drawdown management, risk budgeting
- Key: "Nothing overly technical" means they want judgment and communication, not formulas. Explain concepts like you're talking to a smart non-quant.

ROUND 3: Quant Researcher (maths, stats, coding)

What they said: Maths, stats, coding — nothing too high level.

What they're testing:
- Probability and statistics fundamentals (distributions, hypothesis testing, correlation vs causation)
- Basic coding ability (Python — data manipulation, maybe a simple problem)
- Mathematical reasoning (not proof-writing, but can you think quantitatively?)
- Can you collaborate with quants — do you speak their language?

How to prepare:
- File 11 (Technical Fundamentals) — probability, statistics, Python, SQL
- File 15 (Derivatives Deep Dive) — Part 5 real interview questions (option pricing, Greeks, hedging math)
- File 07 (Risk Reference) — Greek formulas, VaR math
- "Nothing too high level" = they won't ask you to derive Black-Scholes or implement a Monte Carlo from scratch. More likely: "If I give you a distribution with mean X and vol Y, what's the probability of losing more than Z?" or "Write Python to compute rolling correlations."

ROUND 4 (FINAL): Constantine (Head of Discretionary Risk) + his senior

What they said: Q&A, review, final assessment. Constantine was brought in specifically to build this team and is "doing very well."

What they're testing:
- Strategic fit — does this person align with what we're building?
- Leadership potential — will they grow into a senior risk manager?
- Final vetting — any red flags across previous rounds?
- "Do I want this person on my team?"

How to prepare:
- File 04 (Squarepoint Research) — know the firm deeply
- File 17 (Questions to Ask) — Round 4 section. Ask strategic questions about the build-out.
- Have a clear articulation of: what you bring, what you'd do in the first 90 days, and why you specifically for this team
- Constantine built this team from scratch. Acknowledge that — "I understand you were brought in to build the discretionary risk function, and that builder mentality is exactly what I want to be part of."
- Be ready for: "After meeting the team, what questions do you have?" (have real ones)
