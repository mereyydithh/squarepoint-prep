M — DISCRETIONARY VS SYSTEMATIC — CHEAT SHEET

The Two Paradigms — Quick Compare

Systematic Discretionary
Decisions by Algorithms Humans (PMs)
Position count 500–5,000+ 20–80
Holding period Microseconds–weeks Days–months
Edge Speed, data, statistical patterns Fundamental insight, variant perception
Key risk Model failure, crowding, tech Behavioral bias, concentration, thesis refitting
Scales? Not easily (alpha decays with AUM) Better (fewer positions)

Risk Management — How It Diverges

Dimension Systematic RM Discretionary RM
Primary tool Dashboards, kill switch Conversations, judgment
Interaction Engineer-to-system Human-to-human
Override freq Rare (emergencies) Daily (sizing, thesis checks)
Stop-loss Algorithmic, no discretion Framework + discussion
Factor control Enforced by optimizer Monitored, PM may intentionally tilt
Behavioral monitoring N/A (no human bias) Core function
Key nightmare Quant quake (Aug 2007) Single-name blowup, revenge trading

Systematic RM Focus Areas

Focus Detection
Model risk Rolling Sharpe, signal decay curves

Tail risk MC with fat tails, historical stress
Tech risk Uptime, circuit breakers, data quality
Liquidity Slippage: backtest vs realized
Correlation breakdown Real-time vs model assumptions

Discretionary RM Focus Areas

Focus Detection
Position sizing Size vs risk budget
Concentration Single-name %, sector %, geography %
Factor drift Weekly Barra/Axioma decomposition
Behavioral bias P&L distribution, hold periods, thesis audit
Drawdown Escalation ladder (green→stop-out)
Correlation Pairwise + PCA — "you have ONE bet, not eight"
Thesis validity Monthly audit vs original

Squarepoint — Why Both Engines

Reason Detail

Opportunity capture Models can't do activist, special sits, qualitative judgment
LP demand Allocators want diversified alpha sources
Regime robustness When models break, discretionary may still work

Cross-Desk Correlation — The Hidden Risk

  • Run weekly regression of disc returns vs systematic factor loadings
  • If R² > 0.3 against any factor → NOT diversified, DOUBLED up
  • Solutions: factor overlay hedge, shift to idiosyncratic positions, or CRO reduces firm gross

Quick-Fire Interview Answers

  • Why discretionary RM at a systematic firm? "Judgment matters most day-to-day. I use the quant toolkit (factor decomp, stress tests, correlation) to support a PM where each position has a story, catalyst, and thesis I can audit. Quantitative rigor + human judgment intersection."
  • Cross-desk correlation? "Weekly regression of disc returns vs systematic factors. R² > 0.3 → doubled up, not diversified. Present data to PM + CRO with solutions: factor hedge, idiosyncratic shift, or gross reduction."
  • Biggest challenge? "Credibility both ways. CRO wants quant language; PM wants behavioral intelligence. Infrastructure built for 3,000 positions needs qualitative overlays for 40. Supplement VaR with stress + scenario + thesis audit."
