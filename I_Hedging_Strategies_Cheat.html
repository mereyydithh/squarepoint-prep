I — HEDGING STRATEGIES — CHEAT SHEET

ONE-LINERS

Concept One-liner
No free hedge Every form of protection extracts payment in some currency — premium, upside, basis risk, complexity, or illiquidity. The RM's job is to make the currency explicit.
Diagnose first Before picking an instrument, diagnose the disease. A put doesn't help if the risk is basis. A futures hedge doesn't help if the risk is convexity. Match the hedge to the SPECIFIC risk.
Static vs dynamic Static hedging is "set and forget." Dynamic hedging is "adjust as conditions change." Both have costs — dynamic costs execution/slippage; static costs missed optimization opportunities.
Greek impact Adding a hedge doesn't just reduce risk — it changes your ENTIRE Greek profile. A put adds gamma, theta, vega, and cross-Greeks that interact with the existing book.
Roll cost Rolling a hedge is not neutral — you're closing one position at a loss (theta decayed) and opening a new one at market price (potentially higher IV). The roll is where hedge cost compounds.
False confidence A hedge that doesn't perform in stress is worse than no hedge — because it created FALSE CONFIDENCE that the position was protected.

THE FIVE TRADEOFFS (EVERY HEDGE HAS A PRICE)

Tradeoff Tension PM question
Cost vs protection Better protection = more premium "How much drag can your Sharpe absorb?"
Precision vs liquidity Single-name = perfect but expensive; Index = cheap but basis risk "Exact coverage or directional protection?"
Horizon mismatch Position = no expiry; Hedge = expires → roll costs "Event (1 week) or regime (6 months)?"
Upside surrender Collars/call overwriting cap gains "Max return you'd accept for zero premium?"
Timing Cheapest when you don't need it; most expensive when you do "Pay insurance when the sun is shining?"

DECISION FRAMEWORK — 5 QUESTIONS

  1. What SPECIFIC risk? → Delta, vega, gamma, DV01, credit, concentration, tail, basis, liquidity
  2. What horizon? → 1-5 days (weekly options) | 1-4 weeks (monthlies) | 1-6 months (quarterlies) | 6M+ (LEAPs/rolling)
  3. Upside tolerance? → None (puts) | Capped (collar) | Partially capped (put spread)
  4. Premium budget? → Max ~15% of expected alpha for hedge cost
  5. Basis risk accepted? → R² > 0.90 (excellent) | 0.70-0.90 (good) | < 0.70 (reconsider instrument)

KEY FORMULAS

Beta hedge sizing:
$$N = \frac{V_p \times (\beta_{\text{current}} - \beta_{\text{target}})}{V_f}$$

Optimal hedge ratio:
$$h^* = \rho \times \frac{\sigma_{\text{position}}}{\sigma_{\text{hedge}}}$$

Hedge effectiveness:
$$R^2 = 1 - \frac{\text{Var(hedged)}}{\text{Var(unhedged)}}$$

Delta-hedged P&L:
$$\text{P\&L} = \frac{1}{2}\Gamma(\Delta S)^2 - \Theta \Delta t$$

Breakeven daily move:
$$\Delta S_{\text{BE}} = \sigma_{\text{implied}} \times S \times \sqrt{\Delta t}$$

Tail hedge EV:
$$\text{EV} = P(\text{tail}) \times \text{Payoff} - (1 - P(\text{tail})) \times \text{Annual cost}$$

COMMON MISTAKES

  1. Hedging too late → Buy when VIX is LOW, not after the sell-off
  2. Over-hedging → Theta drag > alpha = portfolio can't make money
  3. Wrong instrument → R² < 0.70 = find a better instrument
  4. Set-and-forget → Roll calendar with alerts, no coverage gaps
  5. Ignoring roll costs → Monthly puts = 3%+ annualized drag
  6. "Free" collar illusion → Quantify upside surrender in DOLLARS
  7. Wrong Greek → Futures = delta only; puts = delta + gamma + vega
  8. Correlation-dependent → Monitor ρ weekly, have Plan B

INTERVIEW ANSWER TEMPLATES

"Hedge concentrated equity?"
→ Framework first (5 questions) → Single-name put (cleanest, expensive) → Put spread (halves cost, caps protection) → Collar (zero cost, caps upside) → Futures (free but basis risk, R² ≈ 0.6 for single name vs index) → Make tradeoff explicit to PM

"When NOT to hedge?"
→ (1) Cost > risk: hedge EV is negative → (2) Hedge adds MORE risk via fragile correlation → (3) PM's thesis IS the risk and it's within limits

"Puts vs futures?"
→ Futures: free, liquid, delta-only, linear, no gap protection
→ Puts: premium cost, delta + gamma + vega, convex, GAP protection, hedge IMPROVES as crash deepens

"How evaluate hedge effectiveness?"
→ R² overall + R²_stress on tail days → If R²_stress << R²_overall, hedge gives false confidence
