R — PRACTICAL RISK JUDGMENT — CHEAT SHEET

5-Minute Blotter Scan (8 Checks)

  1. Concentration → any name > 5% NAV? any sector > 15%?
  2. Direction → net/gross ratio → intentional or drift?
  3. Greeks → short gamma = gets longer when market falls (TOXIC). Short vega = loses on vol spike.
  4. Correlation → are "different" positions the same bet? Run factor decomposition.
  5. P&L trajectory → anything bleeding > 2 weeks? thesis still live?
  6. Hedge effectiveness → does the hedge work in CURRENT regime? Check stock-bond ρ.
  7. Liquidity → can we exit every position < 2 days at < 50bp impact?
  8. Stress → worst single-day scenario → does it breach any limit?

Morning Summary Template

TOP 3 RISKS: [concentration] [gamma profile] [hedge effectiveness]
KEY EVENTS: [data release + consensus]
PM EXPOSURE: [how book reacts to each outcome]
MY RECOMMENDATION: [specific trade + cost-benefit]

Urgency Matrix

Low prob Med prob High prob
High impact (>$5M) MONITOR PREPARE (hedge/IC) ACT NOW
Med impact ($1-5M) ACCEPT WATCH ADDRESS (1-2 days)
Low impact IGNORE ACCEPT ROUTINE

Escalation Ladder

  • 🟢 GREEN: 70-80% of limit → verbal to PM
  • 🟡 YELLOW: breach soft limit → PM + desk head within 1hr
  • 🟠 ORANGE: 90%+ hard limit → PM + desk head + risk committee same day
  • 🔴 RED: hard breach / margin call → everyone up to CRO immediately

Value Spectrum (Be Level 4-5, NOT Level 1-2)

  1. Compliance: "You breached the limit"
  2. Monitoring: "You're at 92%"
  3. Diagnosis: "60% from gamma, 30% from energy"
  4. Solutions: "Option A: roll puts (saves $2.5M VaR, costs $200K). Option B: trim XOM 20%. Your call."
  5. Anticipation: "CPI in 2hrs. If hot, your gamma + energy = $8M loss scenario. Pre-adjust?"

Core Principle

Maximize INFORMED risk-taking. Every $ of risk must be:
Intentional | Sized within budget | Understood at portfolio level | Compensated | Hedgeable

Pre-Trade Checklist (2 min)

VaR impact → Concentration → Margin → Correlation with book → Factor overlap → Liquidity → Worst case

Behavioral Red Flags

Signal Response
"It has to come back" ESCALATE — most dangerous sentence in trading
Adding to loser, no new catalyst "What's changed since entry?"
Thesis changed 3+ times Document all versions → present the timeline
Stop-loss moved lower repeatedly Discipline breakdown → escalate
Position sizes growing after win streak "Trade size up 40%. Deliberate?"
60%+ positions underwater, no catalysts Aggregate concern → flag holistically

Daily Workflow

Phase Key tasks
Pre-market (06:30-09:15) Overnight scan → position check → stress refresh → morning summary
Market hours (09:30-16:00) Real-time P&L + Greeks → PM requests → anomaly detection → event monitoring
Post-close (16:00-18:00) EOD report → P&L attribution → tomorrow's catalysts → scenario update
Weekly Factor decomposition, correlation evolution, alpha vs beta, drawdown analysis

Communication Rules

  1. Lead with P&L, not Greek letters
  2. Be definitive: "I recommend X. Here's why."
  3. Pick battles: flag 1-2 things that MATTER, not 10
  4. Push back framework: validate thesis → add risk dimension → bring data → offer alternatives → escalate if needed
  5. Senior format: Situation → Quantification → Recommendation → Trade-offs (90 sec max)

Margin Management

Utilization Action
< 60% Comfortable
60-75% Monitor
75-85% Flag: "One bad day = margin call"
85-95% Active discussion: what to trim?
> 95% Emergency: act within hours

Liquidity

  • Days to exit = Position / (ADV × participation rate)
  • Normal liquidity: 10% participation ≈ 15-25bp impact
  • Stressed liquidity = Normal × (1/0.3) to (1/0.5) → illiquid positions take 3x longer in crisis
