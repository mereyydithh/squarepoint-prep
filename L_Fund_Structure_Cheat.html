Multi-Strat Pod Economics

PM payout example ($500M allocation):
- Gross P&L: +$30M
- Less costs (financing, tech, data, infra): −$14M
- Net P&L: +$16M
- PM payout (15%): $2.4M

Pod termination triggers:
- −5% drawdown → 50% de-risk, warning
- −10% → full liquidation, stop-out
- Repeated limit breaches → reduced allocation

6 Structural Risks (Not Market Risk)

# Risk Key metric
1 Redemption liquidity Days to liquidate 25% of NAV
2 Leverage fragility Margin buffer ratio
3 PB counterparty PB CDS spread, rehypothecation level
4 Fee incentive PM distance below HWM
5 Pro-cyclical margin Buffer under 2× margin stress
6 Correlation convergence Firm VaR at crisis correlations

The Death Spiral Chain

Market stress → Leveraged P&L loss → PB margin increase → Forced selling → Market impact → LP redemptions → More selling → Repo lines pulled → Fire sale → Fund terminates

RM job: Break the chain at step 2 by maintaining margin buffer and pre-planning liquidation order.

Quick-Fire Interview Answers

  • Structure & risk: "Liquidity mismatch (quarterly redemptions ≠ position liquidity), leverage fragility (3× means 5% move = 15% NAV hit), pro-cyclical margin (PBs tighten exactly when you're losing), HWM behavioral pressure"
  • Margin call: "Three phases: (1) PB notification, (2) respond — post cash, post securities, or reduce positions (sell liquid low-conviction first), (3) aftermath — reassess portfolio for another call"
  • Multi-strat risk: "Pod correlations spike in stress (0.3 → 0.85). Diversification benefit vanishes. Must stress-test firm VaR at crisis correlations, not normal-market."
