F — RISK MEASURES CHEAT SHEET

VaR — VALUE AT RISK

One-liner: VaR is the maximum loss at a given confidence level over a given horizon under NORMAL conditions — and it says absolutely nothing about what happens when conditions stop being normal.

99% 1-day VaR = $5M → "On 99/100 days, loss ≤ $5M. On day 100, it's WORSE — and VaR says nothing about how much worse."

Three Methods

Method How Best for Fatal weakness
Parametric Normal dist × σ × z-score Linear instruments, fast Misses fat tails + nonlinearity (options)
Historical Replay last N days, take percentile No dist assumption Hostage to history. Equal weighting. Ghost effects.
Monte Carlo Simulate 10K+ scenarios, full reprice Options, exotics, complex books Expensive. Model risk. False precision.

z-scores: 95% = 1.645 | 97.5% = 1.96 | 99% = 2.326

Time scaling: VaR_10d ≈ VaR_1d × √10 (crude but standard)

VaR Decomposition

Type What it tells you
Component VaR Position i's contribution to total VaR (sum to total, accounts for diversification)
Marginal VaR ∂VaR/∂wᵢ — how VaR changes if you add $1 to position i
Incremental VaR VaR(with position) − VaR(without position) — full add/remove effect

VaR Limitations — KNOW COLD

  1. Not worst case — threshold only, silent on tail magnitude
  2. Assumes normal conditions — crisis ≠ calm (correlations, liquidity, gaps)
  3. Backward-looking — calm history → low VaR → just when risk is building
  4. Not subadditive — VaR(A+B) can > VaR(A) + VaR(B)
  5. Gameable — sell deep OTM options → low VaR, massive hidden tail
  6. Point-in-time — EOD snapshot misses intraday
  7. Correlation instability — calm-period matrix ≠ crisis matrix

"VaR is a speedometer, not a crash detector."

ES — EXPECTED SHORTFALL (CVaR)

One-liner: ES answers the question VaR refuses to ask — "When we breach VaR, how bad does it actually get?"

$$\text{ES}{99\%} = E[\text{Loss} | \text{Loss} > \text{VaR}{99\%}]$$

Property VaR ES
Tail severity Blind Measures it
Subadditive No Yes (coherent)
Regulatory Old (Basel II/III) New (FRTB → 97.5% ES)
Gameable Easy Much harder

ES/VaR Ratio — Tail Shape Diagnostic

Ratio Tail Typical position
1.0-1.5 Thin/normal Diversified equity, futures
1.5-2.5 Moderate Mixed books
2.5-4.0 Fat — investigate Short gamma, concentrated
4.0+ Cliff — DANGER Short OTM options, short var swap

Normal distribution: ES/VaR = 1.15. Student-t (ν=5): ES/VaR = 1.83. Actual equity markets: 1.4-1.6 diversified, 2.0-5.0+ options books.

Rule: ES/VaR > 3.0 → investigate for hidden concavity.

STRESS TESTING

One-liner: VaR tells you what happens on a normal bad day. Stress tests tell you what happens on the WORST day — the one that actually threatens the fund's survival.

Three Types

Type What Why
Historical Replay GFC, COVID, Volmageddon with current positions Did we survive past crises?
Hypothetical Taiwan conflict, Treasury auction fail, Fed emergency Next crisis ≠ last crisis
Sensitivity grid Factor bump heat map (equity × vol × rates) Where exactly does the portfolio break?

Key Historical Scenarios

Event SPX VIX 10Y rates Credit Oil
GFC 2008 −40% 80+ −200bp +400bp HY −55%
Volmageddon 2018 −10% 14→50 +30bp +50bp −5%
COVID 2020 −34% 14→82 −100bp +600bp HY −65%
Japan carry unwind 2024 Nikkei −12% 38 −30bp +30bp −3%

Reverse Stress Testing

Start from the LOSS (e.g., drawdown limit). Work backward to find the scenario that causes it. If that scenario is plausible → hedge NOW.

FIXED INCOME RISK MEASURES

One-liner: How much money you make or lose for a 1bp move in yield. THE unit of rates risk.

Measure Definition Formula
DV01 P&L per 1bp yield move ModDur × Price × Notional / 10,000
CS01 P&L per 1bp credit spread widening SpreadDur × MV / 10,000
KRD Duration by tenor bucket Captures non-parallel curve moves (steepener/flattener)
JTD Jump-to-default loss (1 − Recovery) × Notional. Concentration risk.

DV01 ladder matters more than total DV01. A portfolio with DV01 = 0 can still have +$50K/bp at 2Y and −$50K/bp at 30Y = massive steepener bet.

PERFORMANCE METRICS

Key: Rp = annualized portfolio return. Rf = risk-free rate (T-bill/OIS). σp = annualized portfolio vol. σ_down = downside deviation (only below-target returns).

Metric Formula What it measures Good level
Sharpe (Rp − Rf) / σp Return per unit of total risk 0.7-2.0 (HF). >3.0 = suspicious.
Sortino (Rp − Rf) / σ_down Return per unit of downside risk Higher than Sharpe = positive skew (good)
Calmar Ann. return / Max DD
IR (Rp − Rb) / TE Consistency of benchmark outperformance >0.5 = good
Omega Gains above threshold / Losses below Full distribution comparison >1.0

Sharpe Gotchas

  1. Symmetric penalty — punishes upside vol equally with downside
  2. Short-vol gaming — selling tail risk → high Sharpe until blowup
  3. Time noise — need ~4 years monthly data to distinguish Sharpe 1.0 from 0
  4. Factor-blind — doesn't separate alpha from beta

Quick diagnostic: Sortino << Sharpe → negative skew hidden → investigate.

Alpha-Beta Decomposition

$$R_p = \alpha + \beta R_m + \epsilon$$

  • β > 0.3 → significant market exposure (not pure alpha)
  • Multi-factor: remove market + size + value + momentum → residual = real alpha
  • PM claims alpha but has β_momentum = 0.8? That's factor exposure, not skill.

RISK BUDGETING

$$\text{Fund VaR budget} = \Sigma \text{ Desk allocations} + \text{Reserve}$$

Concept Definition
Utilization Actual VaR / VaR budget. Normal = 60-90%.
Over-utilization >100%. Requires position cut or IC approval.
Diversification benefit Total fund VaR < sum of desk VaRs (if uncorrelated)
RAROC Desk P&L / VaR allocation. Risk-adjusted performance.

LIQUIDITY RISK

One-liner: A position isn't just risky because it can lose value — it's risky because you might not be able to EXIT when you need to.

$$\text{LVaR} = \text{VaR} + \text{Liquidation Cost}$$

$$\text{Days to Liquidate} = \frac{\text{Position Size}}{\text{ADV} \times \text{Participation Rate (10\%)}}$$

Tier Liquidity DTL VaR treatment
1 (Liquid) ADV > $50M < 3d Standard 1-day VaR
2 (Semi) ADV $5-50M 3-10d √DTL scaling
3 (Illiquid) ADV < $5M 10-30d √DTL + stress spread
4 (Very illiquid) No secondary mkt 30d+ Full position at risk

VaR BACKTESTING

One-liner: A VaR model that's never tested is just an expensive guess. Backtesting asks: "How many times did actual losses exceed VaR?" and if the answer is too many (or too few), the model is broken.

99% VaR, 250 days → expected exceptions: 2.5/year

Exceptions Basel zone Action
0-4 🟢 Green Model OK
5-9 🟡 Yellow Under review. Higher multiplier.
10+ 🔴 Red Model rejected.

Kupiec test: Are exceptions the right NUMBER?
Christoffersen test: Are exceptions INDEPENDENT (not clustered)?
Conditional coverage: Both combined (gold standard).

Result Diagnosis
Too few exceptions VaR too conservative → capital inefficiency
Correct + evenly spaced Well-calibrated
Too many exceptions VaR underestimates risk → dangerous
Clustered exceptions Model doesn't adapt to regimes → switch to EWMA/FHS

MODEL RISK

One-liner: Every risk measure is the output of a model. If the model is wrong, the risk measure is worse than useless — it creates false confidence.

Source Risk
Wrong distribution VaR underestimates tails 2-5×
Stale parameters Correlations from calm → wrong in crisis
Missing risk factors "Unknown unknowns"
Wrong pricing model Greeks wrong → hedges leak
Implementation bugs Most common actual source of failure

P&L explain: Model-predicted P&L vs actual. Persistent gap = model is wrong.

KEY INTERVIEW LINES

VaR limitations: "Speedometer, not crash detector. Five flaws: tail-blind, assumes normality, backward-looking, not subadditive, gameable. Supplement with ES, stress tests, and judgment."

ES vs VaR: "Both have same VaR = $5M. Portfolio A's ES = $34M (short OTM puts — concave). Portfolio B's ES = $6.5M (diversified equity — linear). Only ES reveals Portfolio A is 5× more dangerous."

Stress testing: "Three layers: historical (did we survive 2008?), hypothetical (what if Taiwan?), sensitivity grids (where does P&L break?). Fourth: reverse stress test — start from the loss, find the scenario. If it's plausible, hedge now."

Sharpe: "Standard metric but three fatal flaws: symmetric vol penalty, short-vol gaming, small-sample noise. Always check Sortino — if Sortino << Sharpe, there's negative skew hiding."

Drawdown: "−20% needs +25% to recover. −50% needs +100%. That's why hard limits exist — past −20%, recovery becomes a multi-year project. Past −50%, the fund is done."
