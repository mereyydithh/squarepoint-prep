GDP & Output Gap

  • Y = C + I + G + (X − M) → C = 68%, I = 18%, G = 17%, NX = -3%
  • Output gap = (Y_actual − Y_potential) / Y_potential × 100 → positive = overheating, negative = slack
  • Okun's Law: ΔU ≈ −0.5 × (g − g*) → 1% above-trend growth → UE falls 0.5pp
  • Taylor Rule: r = r_neutral + 0.5(π − π) + 0.5(y − y*) → currently prescribes ~3.25% vs actual 3.75%

Inflation Transmission (Oil Shock)

  1. Gasoline ↑ (immediate) → 2. Transport ↑ (1-2M) → 3. Food ↑ (2-4M) → 4. Input costs ↑ (2-4M)
  2. Consumer prices ↑ (3-6M) → 6. Wage demands (6-12M) → 7. Wage-price spiral (12-18M)
  3. Stages 1-4 = cost-push (transitory) | Stages 5-7 = demand-pull (self-reinforcing)

Four-Quadrant Macro Regime

Growth ↑ Growth ↓
Inflation ↑ REFLATION (equities +, bonds −) STAGFLATION (nothing works except commodities)
Inflation ↓ GOLDILOCKS (best: equities ++, bonds +) DEFLATION (equities −, bonds ++)

Stock-Bond Correlation (CRITICAL)

  • Growth-driven risk → negative correlation → bonds hedge equities → 60/40 works
  • Inflation-driven risk → positive correlation → bonds AND equities sell off → 60/40 fails
  • Monitor rolling 3M correlation; sign flip = reassess ALL hedge ratios

Crisis Pattern

  1. ACCUMULATION → leverage builds, risk premia compress
  2. TRIGGER → shock exposes fragility
  3. CONTAGION → forced selling, correlation → 1.0
  4. POLICY RESPONSE → central bank intervenes
  5. RESOLUTION → vol declines, new regime

RM Quick Checks

  • Every position has embedded macro assumptions — surface them
  • VaR understates risk during regime transitions (correlation instability)
  • Regime determines whether your hedges work or amplify losses
  • Daily: overnight moves → data release preview → release vs consensus → PM exposure map

Fiscal

  • r − g framework: If growth > interest cost → debt ratio stabilizes. If r > g → spirals.
  • US: 6% deficit/GDP + Fed at 3.75% = fiscal-monetary conflict → sticky inflation + higher long-end

FX

  • Carry = r_invest − r_fund − E[ΔFX] → carry trades are short vol (steady until they explode)
  • Dollar smile: USD strong in US exceptionalism OR global crisis; weak in synchronized growth
  • EM vulnerability: twin deficits + FX debt + low real rates = fragile
