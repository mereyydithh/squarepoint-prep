S — OPINION FORMATION — CHEAT SHEET

Trader vs. Risk Manager View

Trader Risk Manager
Question "What WILL happen?" "What COULD happen that's under-priced?"
Output Position Recommendation
Success Right more than wrong Identified risk BEFORE it hit

6-Step Framework

  1. Map consensus — Fed futures, VIX, credit, oil curve, BEI, USD, gold
  2. Find where consensus breaks — binary events, regime shifts, second-order effects, crowding
  3. Quantify asymmetry — $\text{Asym} = \frac{P_{tail} \times Loss_{tail}}{P_{base} \times Gain_{base}}$ → if > 1.0, position is risk-negative
  4. Map to portfolio — which positions, which channel, estimated P&L
  5. Recommend — always present 3 options: reduce / hedge / accept+tighten
  6. Set kill conditions — specific data that makes you change your mind

Asymmetry Worked Example

70% cut (+2% eq), 30% hold (-5% eq)
Asym = (0.30 × 5) / (0.70 × 2) = 1.07 → risk-weighted NEGATIVE → hedge the tail

Quality Spectrum

Level Example Grade
1 Headline "Markets are risky because tariffs" FAIL
2 Observer "VIX 24, spreads widening" — no interpretation WEAK
3 Analyst "RV > IV by 60%, vol underpriced" OK
4 Risk Mgr + connects to book + recommends action GOOD
5 Strategic + regime-aware + cross-PM + cost-benefit + kill condition TARGET

8 Risk Themes To Track

  1. CB policy — futures, dot plot, data surprises
  2. Energy/supply — term structure, shipping, inventories
  3. Credit cycle — CDX IG/HY, leveraged loans, fallen angels
  4. Vol regime — VIX level, term structure, RV vs IV
  5. Positioning — CFTC COT, short interest, ETF flows
  6. FX/USD — DXY, rate diffs, EM FX
  7. Liquidity — Fed balance sheet, repo rates, bid-ask
  8. Earnings — forward EPS revisions, breadth, multiples

Confidence Calibration

Confidence Expression Action
90%+ "Highly confident..." Full recommended hedge
70-90% "Balance of evidence suggests..." Partial hedge + contingency
50-70% "I lean toward X..." Monitor, prepare only
< 50% "I don't have a strong view — here's the framework..." No directional view

Push-Back Responses (Memorize These)

  • "Too bearish" → "Tail impact is 3x base. I'm flagging asymmetry, not direction."
  • "VIX already high" → "RV running 60% above IV. Implied is CHEAP vs realized."
  • "Puts are expensive" → "Put spread costs half. Or collar — cap upside to fund downside."
  • "Thesis is intact" → "Right on direction, still lose on path if vol triggers stops. Size to survive."
  • "Why listen to RM?" → "I see aggregate book. I know cross-PM correlation. That's info you don't have."

Morning 3-Question Drill (90 sec)

  1. What's the biggest risk to the book TODAY? (specific)
  2. Is the market pricing it? (VIX, skew, credit)
  3. What would I recommend IF it materializes? (specific action)

Expressing Views By Context

Setting Time Structure
Morning meeting 2 min "3 things on my radar" + data + implication
PM 1-on-1 5 min Lead with THEIR book → risk → recommendation
Risk committee 10 min Regime → exposure → recommendation → cost
Interview 1-2 min Framework → example → portfolio connection

Opinion Traps

  • Anchoring → set kill conditions in advance
  • Narrative creep → pre-define what disproves your view
  • Complexity crutch → force 1-sentence view with a number
  • Anti-consensus bias → consensus is usually right; find SPECIFIC gaps
  • Risk theater → flag 1-2 things that matter, not 15

When You Don't Know → Uncertainty Template

"I don't have a strong view on X. What I do know:
Range of outcomes is [wide/narrow]. Market prices [Y].
Our exposure to the tails is [$Z].
Regardless of direction, the RM action is [specific]."
