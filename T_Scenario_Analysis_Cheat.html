T — SCENARIO ANALYSIS — CHEAT SHEET

7-Step Scenario Engine

  1. FACTS (2 min) — magnitude, percentile, co-movements, gap vs grind, volume
  2. EXPOSURE (3 min) — direct + indirect (beta, FX, vol, margin, correlation channels)
  3. SCENARIOS (5 min) — Base (50-60%) / Adverse (25-35%) / Extreme (5-15%)
  4. QUANTIFY (5 min) — delta P&L + gamma adj + vega + correlation + margin + liquidity
  5. SENSITIVITY (3 min) — grid across magnitudes → find the BREAKPOINT
  6. COMMUNICATE (3 min) — 1-pager: situation, P&L, 3 scenarios, key risk, recommendation
  7. FOLLOW-UP — time/price/data triggers for next update

Sigma Calibration

$$\sigma_{daily} = \frac{\text{Ann. RV}}{\sqrt{252}}$$
Oil RV 35% → σ = 2.2% → 8% move = 3.6σ (once per 6,000 days)

Reverse Stress Test

"What scenario causes us to lose X% of NAV?" → work backwards from the loss

Sensitivity Matrix (Find the Breakpoint)

Run the shock at 5 magnitudes → find the price where 50% of DD limit is consumed → THAT's your action trigger

5×5 Shock Interaction

When oil shocks: don't just model oil → model oil + equity drag + rate spike + credit widening + VIX jump simultaneously

Regime Matters

Same shock, different regime:
- Risk-on: SPX -3% = dip-buyers arrive, recovers in days
- Risk-off: SPX -3% = cascade to -8%, stops trigger, margin calls

Path-Dependent > Point-in-Time

5 days of -2% with daily margin calls > single -8% crash (forced selling locks in losses)

7 Deadly Sins

  1. Precision theater (false accuracy)
  2. Fighting last war (only past scenarios)
  3. Ignoring non-linearity (gamma, convexity)
  4. Static correlations (use stressed ρ)
  5. Ignoring liquidity (exit costs)
  6. Missing 2nd-order (feedback loops)
  7. No "so what?" (always end with recommendation)
