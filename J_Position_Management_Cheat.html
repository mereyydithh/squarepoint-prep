J — POSITION MANAGEMENT — CHEAT SHEET

ONE-LINERS

Concept One-liner
Sizing The PM decides WHAT to trade. The RM helps decide HOW MUCH. Sizing is the largest single driver of portfolio P&L variance.
Liquidity ceiling You can't size a position larger than you can EXIT. Liquidity is not a soft constraint — it's a hard wall.
Conviction Isn't "I feel strongly." It's a structured assessment of variant perception, catalyst, downside, and edge durability — each of which can break independently.
Risk budget A CONCESSION of trust from the firm. Breach it and you lose trust. Manage it well and you earn more.
RM monitoring role The PM builds the portfolio. The RM monitors whether the portfolio the PM ACTUALLY has matches the portfolio they MEANT to have.
Position lifecycle Every position has a birth, a life, and a death. The RM tracks all three stages with a different lens at each.
Scaling The PM's primary expression of changing conviction. The RM ensures scaling decisions are CONSCIOUS, not reactive.
Risk contribution A 5% position in a high-beta, high-correlation stock might contribute 20% of portfolio VaR. The RM's job is to make RISK CONTRIBUTION visible, not just dollar weight.
Morning dashboard Every morning, the RM should be able to answer 10 questions about the book in under 60 seconds by looking at their dashboard.

SIZING FRAMEWORKS

Framework Formula / Rule Best for
Equal weight $w_i = 1/N$ New desk, unproven PM, high-turnover stat arb
Conviction-based Tier 1: 5-8%, Tier 2: 2-4%, Tier 3: 0.5-2%, Tier 4: 0.25-0.5% Discretionary PM with structured conviction
Vol-weighted Notional = Target risk / σ_i Equal risk contribution per position
Kelly $f^* = (pb - q)/b$ → use ¼ or ½ Kelly in practice Theoretical ceiling on sizing
Max-loss Notional = Acceptable loss / stop-loss distance (%) Risk-budget-driven sizing

Vol-weighted quick example: $200K risk budget per position → JNJ (15% vol) gets $1.33M, TSLA (50% vol) gets $400K.

CONVICTION SCORING — 4 PILLARS

Pillar Question If missing →
Variant perception What do I see the market doesn't? No edge
Catalyst What event + WHEN? Dead money
Downside How much can I lose? Position unsized
Edge durability Why won't it get arbed away? Already priced

Score: 4/4 → Tier 1 | 3/4 → Tier 2 | 2/4 → Tier 3 | 1/4 → Tier 4 | 0/4 → Reject

THREE BROKEN-THESIS TESTS

  1. Is the thesis intact? → If yes, hold/add
  2. Has NEW INFORMATION arrived? → If yes, reassess from scratch (price movement alone ≠ new info)
  3. Would you put this on TODAY? → If no, the position is alive only because of anchoring. CUT IT.

LIQUIDITY-ADJUSTED SIZING

$$\text{Max notional} = \text{ADV}(\$) \times \text{Participation rate (15\%)} \times \text{Max exit days}$$

Check Threshold
Days to exit (normal) < 5 days
Days to exit (stress @ 0.5× ADV) < 10 days
Position as % of ADV < 100%
Estimated impact < 1% one-way
Ownership % of float < 5%

Market impact: $\text{Impact} \approx \sigma \times \sqrt{Q/V} \times k$

EXPOSURE METRICS

Metric Formula
Gross |L| + |S|
Net L − S
Net/Gross Net / Gross (0 = neutral, 1 = fully long)
Beta-adjusted net Σ(w_i × β_i) — true directional exposure

Trap: PM says "I'm modestly net long at $30M" but β-adjusted net = $43M because longs have higher beta than shorts.

RISK DECOMPOSITION

$$\text{Component VaR}i = w_i \times \beta{i,p} \times \text{VaR}_p$$

Key insight: A 15% dollar-weight position with high vol + high correlation can be 33% of portfolio VaR. The RM makes RISK CONTRIBUTION visible, not just dollar weight.

POSITION LIFECYCLE — RM CHECKS

Stage RM checks
Entry Risk budget, marginal VaR, correlation, factor exposure, liquidity (days to exit), conviction tier match, crowding, hedge plan
Hold Daily: P&L + VaR. Weekly: thesis + factors + correlation. Monthly: full review.
Exit (profit) Challenge disposition effect (cutting winners too early)
Exit (loss) Run 3 broken-thesis tests
Exit (forced) Limit breach, liquidity event, compliance, redemption

SCALING RULES

Adding: Only if (1) thesis confirmed by new data, (2) risk budget has room, (3) PM pre-planned the scale-in
Trimming: When (1) valuation target hit, (2) position grew beyond conviction tier, (3) concentration risk, (4) risk budget constraints

Red flag: PM averaging down without passing broken-thesis tests = loss aversion disguised as conviction

INTERVIEW ANSWER TEMPLATES

"PM at 85% risk budget wants new position?"
→ Check MARGINAL VaR → If diversifying (low ρ), might barely move budget → If correlated, could push to 95% → Make tradeoff explicit → "Trim something or accept the risk?"

"How know position is too large?"
→ 3 lenses: (1) Risk contribution >25% of VaR = portfolio IS that position, (2) >5 days to exit = illiquid, (3) Stop-loss would consume >30% of monthly loss budget

"How manage a drawdown?"
→ Structured escalation: 25% = conversation, 50% = restrictions, 75% = defense mode, 100% = stop-out

"PM keeps averaging down?"
→ Show data: (1) position grew from Tier 2 to Tier 1 by price drop not conviction, (2) risk contribution doubled, (3) needs 15% recovery just for breakeven → PM must pass 3 broken-thesis tests to justify
