Fund What killed it Category

Counterparty Risk — Key Concepts

Concept Definition
CVA Price of counterparty credit risk: EE × PD × LGD
DVA Price of YOUR credit risk to them (perverse: deterioration → accounting gain)
FVA Cost of funding collateral (funding spread − OIS × duration)
PFE Max expected exposure at 95% confidence
Wrong-way risk Exposure ↑ as counterparty creditworthiness ↓
Netting ISDA — net across all trades with same CP → reduces exposure 50-90%
CSA Collateral agreement: frequency, threshold, eligible collateral, haircuts

CVA Quick Formula

$$\text{CVA} \approx \text{EE} \times \text{PD} \times \text{LGD}$$

Example: $15M EE × 10% cumulative PD × 60% LGD = $900K CVA charge

ISDA/CSA Key Parameters

Parameter Typical RM concern
Valuation frequency Daily Less frequent = gap risk
Threshold $0–$10M Higher = more unsecured exposure
MTA $250K–$1M Gap between calls
Eligible collateral Cash, G7 bonds Non-cash → haircuts apply
Rehypothecation Yes/No Yes → CP risk on YOUR collateral

Knight Capital — 45-Minute Death

  • Bad code deployed to 1 of 8 servers
  • Old "Power Peg" function: buy at ask, sell at bid (opposite of normal)
  • 28 minutes before anyone noticed
  • $440M loss in 45 min (firm had $365M capital)
  • Lessons: Kill switch, real-time P&L alerts, automated deployment, remove dead code

Regulatory Landscape — What Affects a Hedge Fund

Regulation Key impact on HF
Basel III/IV + SLR PB capital constraints → less leverage, higher financing cost
FRTB PBs: ES replaces VaR, illiquid instruments cost more → flows to HF
Dodd-Frank Volcker Rule, CCP mandates, Form PF reporting
EMIR EU clearing mandate, trade reporting, bilateral margin rules
MiFID II Best execution, research unbundling, transparency
AIFMD EU authorization, leverage reporting, depositary requirement
13F Long equity disclosure (but shorts + TRS = blind spot = Archegos)

FRTB: Old vs New

Feature Old (VaR) FRTB
Risk metric VaR (99%, 10d) ES (97.5%, variable horizon)
Tail risk Ignored beyond VaR Captured (avg loss beyond)
Liquidity Single 10d horizon 10d (liquid) to 120d (illiquid)
P&L test None Must prove model ≈ actual P&L

13F Reporting Gap

Captures Misses
Long equity positions Short positions

Manager-level aggregate Cross-PB exposure

Archegos exploited ALL three gaps → $10B bank losses.

Quick-Fire Interview Answers

  • Beyond market risk: "Counterparty (managed via ISDA/CSA/CCP, watch wrong-way risk), Operational (segregation of duties + 3-way recon + kill switch), Regulatory (Basel impacts PB capacity; 13F gap enabled Archegos)"
  • Monitor counterparty: "Daily: net exposure, collateral coverage, CDS spreads. Structural: ISDA netting, CSA margin, clearing. At inception: wrong-way risk screen"
  • Operational controls: "Segregation of duties, daily 3-way recon, independent pricing, 4-eye principle, real-time P&L alerts with automatic circuit breakers"
