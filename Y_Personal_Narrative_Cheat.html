Stress-Testing Model

  • How: Shocks to flat price, curve shape, vol, basis → recalc P&L using delta/gamma/vega/DV01
  • Why position-level: Aggregate hides basis risk (long crude / short gas looks flat)
  • Validation: Replay COVID March 2020 (within 5%), cross-check Bloomberg MARS, sensitivity analysis
  • Best catch: Liberation Day (Apr 2 2025) — tariffs blew out US vs. international commodity basis. Position-level flagged clients with cross-border aluminum/steel hedges exposed to new tariff-driven basis. Flagged morning of Apr 3, restructured before Apr 9 escalation. Avoided ~$3M MTM losses

SOFR Skew Scanner

  • Output: OI by strike/maturity, day-over-day change, net dealer gamma heatmap, skew metrics
  • Gamma pin: Strike where dealer gamma = 0. Below = short gamma (amplifies vol — dealers sell into declines, buy into rallies). Above = long gamma (dampens vol). Danger = crossing from long-gamma into short-gamma territory.
  • Net dealer gamma calc: Assume dealers short options → gamma × OI × multiplier, aggregate by strike

SABR (Natixis)

  • 4 params: α (vol level), β (CEV backbone, FIXED at 0.5 for rates), ρ (skew, spot-vol correlation), ν (vol-of-vol, controls wings)
  • Calibration: Levenberg-Marquardt, minimize squared error vs market swaption vols. β fixed before calibration.
  • Result: 45bps → 30bps RMSE. Matters for: accurate Greeks + PLAT compliance
  • Limitations: (1) Per-expiry only — no joint surface fit. (2) Wings: Hagan approx breaks down for deep OTM → arbitrage violations. (3) Negative rates: need Free Boundary SABR for EUR swaptions post-2015.
  • Why SABR over Heston for rates: Analytical (closed-form) = millisecond repricing. Heston needs numerical integration (Fourier) = slower. Speed wins for rates desks pricing full swaption grids in real time.
  • PLAT thresholds: Spearman correlation test + ratio test. Green = pass both. Amber = fail one ≤20% of days over 12mo. Red = lose IMA status → Standardised Approach → 2-4× higher capital.
  • NMRFs: Risk factors with <24 verifiable prices in 12 months → punitive stress scenario add-on instead of model-based ES. Fix: improve data sourcing to reclassify as modellable.
  • Bridge back: "...but the most impactful risk work was at BNP NYC"

  • Q4 2025: Turned positive = 3σ event. Cause: central bank gold buying (de-dollarization)

FRTB-SA / SBM Recalibration (€5M Capital Relief)

  • SBM = 3 charges: Delta risk + Vega risk + Curvature risk. Summed, no diversification across the three.
  • Delta/Vega: Sensitivities to prescribed risk factors → aggregated with regulatory correlation matrices across buckets. Three correlation scenarios (low/medium/high) → take max charge.
  • Curvature: Full reval under ±shocks to each risk factor → worst-case net P&L.
  • What I fixed: (1) IR delta was mapped to wrong tenor buckets → inflated charge by ~€2M. (2) Commodity cross-bucket correlation was double-counted → ~€1.5M. (3) Curvature shocks used flat ±1% instead of risk-class-specific sizes → ~€1.5M.
  • Result: €42M → €37M = €5M relief (12%). At 12-15% ROE target → €600-750K annual earnings capacity freed.
  • SA limitations: Static correlations (ignore regime), no cross-asset diversification, systematically 1.5-3× more conservative than IMA.

TECHNICAL PROBES QUICK-REF

Asian Options

  • Payoff: max(A − K, 0) where A = arithmetic average over option life
  • Why corporates love them: smooths price spikes (jet fuel hedging over a year, not one day)
  • Pricing: No closed-form (avg of log-normals ≠ log-normal). Use Turnbull-Wakeman or Monte Carlo.
  • Turnbull-Wakeman: Moment-matching. Fit log-normal with same mean + variance as true arithmetic average → plug into BS. Fast, accurate for vanilla structures.
  • Fixed vs floating strike: Fixed = max(A−K, 0). Floating = max(S_T−A, 0) — pays if final spot > average (captures momentum).

Greeks Extended

Greek Definition Key risk
Delta ∂V/∂S Directional exposure
Gamma ∂²V/∂S² Re-hedging cost, spikes near expiry ATM
Vega ∂V/∂σ Vol exposure
Theta ∂V/∂t Daily time decay, accelerates √T near expiry
Vanna ∂²V/∂S∂σ Delta changes as vol moves — crash risk
Volga ∂²V/∂σ² Vega convexity. Deep OTM = high volga. Vol spike → vega shifts.

Theta non-linear: Near expiry, ATM theta ∝ 1/√T — accelerates sharply. Worst case: short ATM near expiry in quiet market.

Derivatives Pricing

  • BS intuition: Delta-hedge portfolio is riskless → earns risk-free rate → BS PDE. Closed-form = N(d1), N(d2).
  • Itô's lemma: Chain rule for stochastic processes. df = (∂f/∂t + μ∂f/∂X + ½σ²∂²f/∂X²)dt + σ∂f/∂X dW. The bold term = Itô correction = gamma in options.
  • Risk-neutral pricing: Change of measure so every asset earns r. Drift disappears → no expected return input needed. Price = E^Q[payoff × discount].
  • Delta hedging = dynamic replication: Continuously rebalance (Δ shares + cash) to replicate option payoff. Cost of replication = option premium.

DV01 & Duration

  • DV01: ModDur × Price × 0.0001. Example: ModDur=8, $1M face → DV01 = $800/bp. Additive across positions.
  • Macaulay duration: Weighted avg time to cash flows (years)
  • Modified duration: Macaulay / (1 + y/m) = % price change per unit yield change
  • Convexity correction: ΔP ≈ −ModDur×Δy + ½×Convexity×(Δy)². Always positive for plain bonds — good to own.
  • Negative convexity: Callable bonds + MBS. Issuer/borrower calls when rates fall → you're short a call → price capped on rally. VaR underestimates risk for MBS in falling rate environments.
