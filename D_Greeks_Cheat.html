D — GREEKS CHEAT SHEET

ONE-LINERS & ANALOGIES

Greek One-liner Analogy
Delta (Δ) Dollars gained/lost per $1 move in underlying. Your effective stock-equivalent position. Volume knob — controls how loud the stock plays through to your P&L. ITM=cranked to 10. ATM=halfway. OTM=barely a whisper. Gamma turns the knob.
Gamma (Γ) How fast delta changes. Acceleration of P&L. THE most important Greek for risk. Airbag — long Γ deploys on impact (cushions + profits from crashes). Short Γ = airbag ripped out (damage accelerates).
Theta (Θ) Daily rent for holding optionality. Long options pay it, short options earn it. Parking meter — ticks slowly far from expiry, eats quarters near expiry. Are you feeding it or do you own it?
Vega (ν) P&L per 1-point change in implied vol. Your exposure to fear itself. Weather forecast, not the weather — you profit from the forecast getting scarier, not the storm itself.
Rho (ρ) P&L per 1% change in interest rates. Minor for short-dated, material for LEAPS/FX. River current — short kayak trip = barely notice; multi-day expedition = carries you miles off course.
Vanna How delta shifts when vol moves. Your hedges get stronger or weaker when fear arrives. Shape-shifter — directional exposure morphs when vol regime changes.
Volga How vega changes when vol moves. Long volga = tail protection (vol convexity). Vol convexity — does a vol spike make things better or worse? Progressively.
Charm Delta drifts overnight from time passing. Critical near expiry and over weekends. Silent thief — steals your hedge overnight while you sleep.
Speed How gamma changes with spot. Important near barriers. Gamma of gamma — is your gamma itself stable or about to shift?
Color How gamma evolves with time. Gamma spikes into expiry. Gamma's clock — measures how fast gamma concentrates approaching expiry.
Dollar delta Δ × contracts × multiplier × spot. The REAL number — not option delta. —
Dollar gamma P&L for 1% move from gamma = ½ × Γ$ × (%move)². Squared → accelerating. —
Gamma/theta Efficiency metric: how much gamma per dollar of theta. Low = overpaying for optionality. —

FIRST-ORDER GREEKS — FORMULAS

Greek Formula Units
Delta ∂V/∂S $/$ (per $1 move)
Gamma ∂²V/∂S² = ∂Δ/∂S Δ per $1 move
Theta ∂V/∂t $/day
Vega ∂V/∂σ $ per 1 vol pt
Rho ∂V/∂r $ per 1% rate

Dollar gamma P&L = ½ × Γ$ × (%ΔS)² → always positive for long Γ, always negative for short Γ

GREEKS BY POSITION — KNOW COLD

Greek Long Call Short Call Long Put Short Put
Δ + (0 to +1) − (0 to −1) − (−1 to 0) + (0 to +1)
Γ + − + −
Θ − (pay) + (earn) − (pay) + (earn)
ν + − + −
ρ + − − +

Universal pattern:
- LONG any option → +Γ, +ν, −Θ (always)
- SHORT any option → −Γ, −ν, +Θ (always)
- Only Δ and ρ differ between calls and puts

WHERE GREEKS PEAK

Greek Highest when Lowest when
Gamma ATM + near expiry + low vol OTM/ITM + far expiry
Theta ATM + near expiry OTM/ITM + far expiry
Vega ATM + far expiry Near expiry (time too short for vol to matter)

Key insight: Want gamma? → Buy near-dated ATM. Want vega? → Buy far-dated ATM. Different tenors, different Greeks.

WHAT MOVES DELTA

Factor Call Δ Put Δ
Spot ↑ → 1 (more ITM) → 0 (less ITM)
Spot ↓ → 0 (more OTM) → −1 (more ITM)
Vol ↑ OTM Δ rises, ITM Δ falls (compress to 0.50) Same compression
Vol ↓ OTM Δ falls, ITM Δ rises (polarize to 0 or 1) Same polarization
Time passes Same polarization as lower vol Same

GAMMA-THETA TRADEOFF

Profile Gamma Theta P&L shape Risk
Long Γ, short Θ + − Many small losses, occasional big wins Prolonged low vol → bleed to death
Short Γ, long Θ − + Many small wins, occasional BIG losses One tail event wipes months of income

No free lunch: Cannot have +Γ AND +Θ with vanilla options.

Break-even: Gamma scalping breaks even when realized vol = implied vol.

SECOND-ORDER GREEKS

Greek Formula One-liner Key insight
Vanna ∂Δ/∂σ Shape-shifts your delta when fear arrives — hedges get stronger or weaker at the worst moment. Puts become better hedges in a vol spike. Drives dealer flows.
Volga ∂ν/∂σ Convexity of your vol bet — vol spike makes things progressively better or worse. Long OTM wings = long volga = tail protection (vol convexity)
Charm ∂Δ/∂t Silent thief — steals your delta hedge overnight while nothing moves. Delta-hedged Friday → unhedged Monday. Critical for 0DTE.
Speed ∂Γ/∂S Gamma of gamma — is your gamma stable or about to shift? Important near barriers
Color ∂Γ/∂t Gamma's clock — how fast gamma concentrates into expiry. Gamma spikes into expiry

VOL-SPOT CORRELATION

SPX ↓ 1% → VIX ↑ ~2-4 pts (avg)
SPX ↓ 5% → VIX ↑ ~15-25 pts
SPX ↑ 5% → VIX ↓ ~5-8 pts (asymmetric!)

Short vega is correlated with long delta. Both hurt in a sell-off. Compounding losses.

KEY INTERVIEW LINES

Gamma: "Gamma tells you WHERE YOU'LL BE after a move. Delta tells you where you are NOW. For risk, gamma matters more."

Gamma-theta: "No free lunch. Long gamma = pay daily rent for crash protection. Short gamma = earn rent but write tail insurance. RM checks: is the tradeoff fair? Is the gamma/theta ratio efficient?"

Second-order: "Vanna makes your put hedges MORE effective in a crash (delta drifts in your favor). Volga makes short OTM puts INCREASINGLY dangerous (vega loss accelerates). These drive dealer flows that move billions daily."

Vol crush: "Buying straddles into events: you need the realized move to exceed the implied move. If IV was 25% and realized is 10%, you lose on vega crush despite being 'right' about the event."
